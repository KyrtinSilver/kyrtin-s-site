I have an open question for my network, relating in particular to those engineers and innovators looking to push the frontiers of AI. It isn’t about you, but the person next to you. Who do you work with that you can say works well with others?

This is a question of Collective Intelligence (CI), since beyond simple tricks like raw averages and median values among crowds, what drives CI is the ability to work cooperatively, as a team that exceeds the capacities of any one member. This team requires a stable and reasonable baseline of trust, both in the capabilities and intentions of other constituent members, as well as the will to work together (as peers, not followers).

If I were to sift through all of my connections and followers then I’d find many people doing some vaguely “cognitive” work with varying degrees of relation to technological innovation, but not all of those people work well with others. All else being equal, the deciding factor for what actually makes a difference in the world is usually the presence or absence of that ability being put into practice.

My co-founder and I (as well as our broader team) are an example of two very different specializations with very different perspectives, where we can still reliably sit down for any given meeting and iron out a solution within a few minutes. This is what happens in any functional CI, cooperation, and increasingly intelligent solutions.

There are plenty of people making the claim that they have single-handedly solved “AGI” or some similar proxy by themselves (and increasingly, claiming the help of sycophantic trashbots), but part of the reason why such individuals never accomplish anything of substance is because they don’t work well with others, so others never correct their fundamental errors. Sometimes they collect followers, but not peers.

This is why I’m asking the question of peers. Who do you know that is dedicated to pushing the frontiers of AI AND who works well with such peers, not just followers?

This is one of my rare posts that is intended for humans, at least in-so-far as it is about how people reply, and who they name.

Although integrations and broader collaboration with ICOM-based systems remain impractical pre-funding, there are other interesting technologies and methods (with different fundamental capacities) that people could collaborate on the development, demonstration, and integration of.

If there is sufficient interest and a sufficient group of cooperative innovators, then I’ll gladly connect the dots. Building CI within groups is vastly easier than building it from scratch, and my team has experience doing both, so it doesn’t take much effort to build the former when all of the pieces are there.

Hopefully, this will receive more than cricket noises, though that too could be telling.
