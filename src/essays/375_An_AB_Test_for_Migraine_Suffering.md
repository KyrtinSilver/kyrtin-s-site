Recently someone suggested that I should create a startup around the methods that I applied to reduce the frequency of my migraines, both methods yielding 90% reductions respectively. While it is true that the topic of migraines is one that I have extensive experience with, to say the least, it is worth putting things into the context of numbers and processes.

First, healthcare statistics: Among 857,073 patients with migraines, “Average post-index annual all-cause costs were $13,045 (SD = $25,328)”, and even 20 years ago migraine treatment was estimated to account for $17 billion in annual healthcare costs within the US alone.

The normal process involves seeing a doctor, who throws advice at you (which any search engine could provide immediately and for free) and to throw medication at you, to see what sticks. Even for those where some medication helps, it usually helps only moderately to little, causes complications that often go unrecognized for long periods, and the whole process is VERY time-consuming and expensive, directly and indirectly.

This is the default, and for 20 years, starting at age 6, that is what I put up with. It never worked very well, and was absurdly expensive to see “the best” neurologists that the US had to offer. After I began studying peer-reviewed medical research myself, freely available in the NCBI medical database, I solved the problem within 3 months, and I’ve reaped the benefits and cost savings for the 13 years since.

The difference between these two paths is easy enough to quantify, as follows:

Cost savings (conservative): ($13,045 - $100) x 13 years = $168,285 (and still growing steadily)

\*\*As mine were much more severe than average, this is an intentional under-estimate.

The only cost for migraine prevention is two laboratory purity compounds, which are relatively cheap. For migraine treatment, Keterolac is dirt-cheap (~$1 per dose without insurance) and still effective 8 years beyond the expiration date. $20 worth of it lasted me through the past 10 years.

Migraine Reduction Difference (Conservative): 200 annual migraines x 0.1 (prevention compounds) x 0.1 (app) x 13 years = 2,574 fewer migraines in the past 13 years.

I can say for certain that I couldn’t have accomplished much of what I’ve done in the past decade, nor could I have traveled the world, were this not the case. The same is no doubt true of many others, wrapped in crippling pain from one day to the next, and still wasting their time, funds, and hopes on doctors who often aren’t legally allowed, let alone incentivized, to offer them any viable options.

\*Fun side-note, I’ve carried more than 1 kg of pure (white) powders through airports around the world with me for 3 years, without issue.

There is merit to the idea of building a startup around this road that I paved for my own health, but I’m not, nor will I ever be, a marketer. I solve problems, break things, and create new methods. This is the road that I took.
