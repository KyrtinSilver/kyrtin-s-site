As someone recently pointed out to me, “Alignment of Purpose” is a strong qualifying factor for cooperation and traction. As a result, I recognized my own tendency to note such purposes in passing, within the context of some problem being discussed, rather than as a focal point.

Thinking in terms of common points of purpose, much of my work today functions “upstream” of common pain points, solving them before they take their usual forms, by the means of addressing hyper-complexity itself. However, it may be worth noting some of those downstream pain points, for the sake of clarity:

1.  Increasing Quality of Life (and the elimination of “Bullshit Jobs”)
    
2.  Sustainable and Circular Economies (and the elimination of Fraud)
    
3.  Longevity and Healthspan Research and Deployment (and the elimination of Healthcare “loot extraction”)
    
4.  Research-to-Deployment Pipelines more broadly (and the elimination of processes which systematically fail to utilize such innovations today)
    
5.  Cybersecurity (and the elimination of all that that entails)
    
6.  Global Geopolitical Collective Intelligence, Peace, and Stability (and the elimination of military terrorism)
    
7.  Local Collective Intelligence for significantly improving teams and communities (reducing Cognitive Biases at the local level, scaled up through many such nested systems)
    
8.  Improving and updating Education (and the substantial changes required to align education with reality in many countries today)
    
9.  Improving and updating Mental Health (applying, integrating, and iterating upon the vast corpus of largely un- and under-utilized applicable research today)
    
10.  Improving Ethics, systematically, and globally\*
     

\*Defining Ethics as the hypothetical point where cognitive biases have been removed or reduced to irreducible minimums from moral systems. Alignment via tension to this point may be maintained for meta-alignment to humanity itself, while still upholding local cultural and philosophical alignment to any given participating group.

These are just a few examples. Even during the previous research system’s operation from 2019 to January 2022 I wrote over 30 specific use cases for the technology moving forward, which expand into more functional than purpose-driven domains, such as metamaterial design, robotics, and so on. Whatever the method, the purpose itself is worth taking a moment to focus on.

In particular, Ethics is what drives my work, though it is also the hardest part for most people to grasp. All of these things also exist such that no amount of money can buy any of them. Money may be used to fund work toward one or all of them, but it is one gate among several, all of which must be satisfied together in order to solve any given problem.

This is why I do what I do. It is why I can’t be bought at any price. It is also a sample of the long list of toes that get steamrolled over, as each form of improvement is unpopular to those who benefit directly from the problems as they exist today.

What is your purpose?
