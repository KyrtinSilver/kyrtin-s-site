For years, developers have wanted the opportunity to get involved with the technology that my startup works with. It is time to make that opportunity, in the form of a small project.

The project concept is fairly simple, a less advanced version of a human-like motivational system (relative to ICOM v7) that is compatible with “agents”. Such a motivational system follows a set of dynamics more akin to Chaos Theory than the narrow optimizers of “Reinforcement Learning” and Stochastic Gradient Descent.

I actually expected that someone would come along and do this with a team of their own for years, as we publicly published a sufficient volume of material on the methods for people to build such weaker systems, compatible with LLMs, but no one ever did.

Even using only the publicly published materials, applying my comprehension of their contents, this is a project for a small team, and certainly less work than many such research and GitHub projects. This protects my company’s IP, while opening up a new set of possibilities for other technology.

In particular, such a small team requires, for the sake of sanity and time efficiency:

• A database expert (also able to work with an off-the-shelf graph database)

• An environment, deployment, and integrations expert (To get the parts working together)

• An architect and data scientist (Myself)

...and it optionally may benefit from:

• A formal mathematician (preferably one who has done work with Chaos Theory)

• A more typical AI researcher/engineer (who actually learned to code, before 2020)

• A Cybersecurity specialist (I have several white-hats in mind for this)

...with post-project vetting from:

• An AI Safety/Ethics researcher (who I’ve spoken with previously)

• Further 3rd party testing via universities

I’ve spent the past week thinking through the necessary architecture, as based exclusively on publicly disclosed elements of ICOM. In some ways, the results could mirror the “Weak Digital Proxies” concept that I previously discussed in a 2020 research paper related to our technology, with applications in actual Democracy and governance more broadly, not to mention being vastly more “agentic” than the so-called “agents” of today.

While the work on ICOM remains bottle-necked on my co-founder I’ll shift my focus to this, should a sufficient team be assembled. As overexcited as half-wit VCs got about “OpenClaw”, not to mention the industry’s most obvious frauds throwing millions at that developer to absorb them, the bar couldn’t be much lower for successful projects within the space to draw funding and high demand for talent today. Even demonstrating such a project in operation on Kaggle could prove quite interesting.

Recommendations and messages with interest are welcome.
