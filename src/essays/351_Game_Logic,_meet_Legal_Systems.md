For example 2, you can consider something that is easy to do given game mechanics in something like Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim:  
  

1.  You sneak around a house or shop and steal all of the food items in it. (Just don’t get caught)  
      
    
2.  You use a mortar and pestle to turn those ingredients into potions. (This clears the “stolen” flag on the ingredient items, as it doesn’t transfer over to resulting crafted items)  
      
    
3.  You sell those potions to the NPCs that you just stole the ingredients from. (As the Underpants Gnomes say, “Profit!”)  
      
    

This abuse of game mechanics should sound very familiar, as it is the model that the tech industry uses to steal copyrighted content, run it through a statistical blender, and then sell it back to the people that it was stolen from. Yes, the most absurd abuses of game mechanics are now the commercial status quo at a global scale.

We can also say with certainty that the tech industry’s leading bad actors are absolutely cognizant of exactly what they’re doing. Take the example of Anthropic buying a single copy of practically every book that they could, destructively scanning them, tearing out one page after another, and then burning the remnants.  
  
That is an extremely unusual, time-intensive, and labor-intensive process compared to the far more typical use of digital copies and OCR, the only justification for which can be intentional manipulation of the flaws in a system. This exploited a legal loophole, like a flaw in game logic, resulting in a process no less absurd than any abuse of game mechanics one might find.

These kinds of loopholes are also growing, as bad actors wedge them open through lobbyists, bribes to officials, and courts obeying those bribed officials, as well as creating new loopholes in legislation and regulation, while the abuse of them has gradually become social expectation. This expectation relaxes social backlash against absurd and overtly criminal behaviors, only rarely being revived through extreme, but statistically unavoidable, events.
