A couple of weeks ago I was joking with someone about the possibilities of AI that is more advanced than trashbots (LLMs and their “agentic” derivatives), and they suggested that AI could be used for matters of sexual health. In a specific sense, this isn’t as laughable an idea as it may first appear (although, no, I won’t be the one building it).

Sexual health falls into the category of topics that people are highly averse to the discussion of, even avoiding typing such content into a search engine to look for answers when they have need of them. It is also a subject where humans tend to frequently lack the right kinds of experience and knowledge, as well as one well they frequently fail to learn certain things. That failure to learn tends to be systematic, caused in part by higher cognitive processes taking an extended break during certain activities. Strong emotions also HEAVILY skew many related processes.

From a perspective of Behavioral Economics, Statistics, and Noise, this produces a greatly elevated probability of choices being made without sufficient knowledge or experience, and with a few hardened blind-spots systematically skewing any attempts to resolve problems.

Of course, it would be idiotic to ask these questions of trashbots, not only because they “Bullshit” 100% of the time (the technical term for what people sometimes Anthropomorphize with the term “Hallucination”), but also because they are vulnerable-by-design, and report all data that they collect to various bad actors. Nobody wants their most personal matters of sexual health to be reported to every bad actor on the planet willing to pay fractions of a penny for it, especially when they’re taking horrible advice from a cheap statistical knockoff of intelligence, which is embarrassing in its own right.

All of that being said, it doesn’t have to be that way, as trashbot AI (LLMs and their derivatives) isn’t the only kind of AI.

If people instead have the opportunity to consult with genuinely intelligent systems that are actually secure, private, and not built for Bullshitting, then those systems may be designed and specialized to not just have a deep understanding of the sexual health literature, but to continue developing that understanding well beyond it over time.

This is true of applying such systems to virtually any field, but it offers acute advantages to subjects that humans are otherwise fearful or more generally averse to discussing with others, or averse to the risk of having it discovered about them. Consider the example of Alan Turing, one of the fathers of computing, who after having his sexual preferences discovered by others was maimed by the very same “authorities” that he had famously aided, a betrayal most foul.

This advantage of aiding to address sensitive subjects is further compounded because those very same focal points of fear and aversion are often temporary, or imagined. If people are free to talk through them and take some better informed measures in relation to them, without the risk of their privacy and security being violated, then many such problems may be resolved without issue, improving the lives of those people, and often those around them, in significant and meaningful ways.

There are plenty of more Cyberpunk-esc possibilities that introducing such capacities may also offer, including some that we joked about like AI beds designed for the “Incels” of the world. However, beyond such maximally absurd individuals, the first step toward anything more advanced than good advice is for people to gain access to and start taking that good advice, building trust, and resolving existing problems.

Only then may they look to the possibilities for the future.
