As is likely evident, I read a lot, but easily the best book (on research) that I’ve come across in 2025 is “Bullshit Jobs” by David Graeber (2018). I can and do recommend it to EVERYONE, since it safely describes 40-50% of the working population today, in many ways that more people need to understand and consciously consider.

This is the highest recommendation I’ll give of any book. Everyone has seen this reality, but few have given it substantial, researched, or well-rounded thought.

I’m having to make additions to my latest paper just for this reference, as it covers many critical dynamics as real-world systems interact, evolve, and frequently maladapt.

“If make-believe play is the purest expression of freedom, then make-believe work, imposed by others, is the purest expression of lack of freedom”

...

“A human being, unable to have a meaningful impact on the world, ceases to exist.” - David Graeber, Bullshit Jobs

He also discusses a pattern where:

1\. There is enforced silence about what is going on

2\. Systems are designed not to be understood

3\. These are pushed onto managers who have no idea what is going on below them, largely because it makes no sense.

Sound familiar? If you’re in the AI domain, it really should.

“GenAI” has itself also fueled an explosion of new “Bullshit Jobs”, like the new wave of virtually slave labor, with RLHF and other labeling for AI, “Content Moderation” shops, or all of the “duct-tapers” trying to fix the fire hose deluge of AI-generated slop code.

“GenAI” has never remotely approached the cutting edge for things like understanding, reasoning, or decision-making, and even the visual Slop generators can only claim such performance at the cost of objectively and demonstrably illegal activity at the largest scales possible, making them deserving of proportionately up-scaled severity in the punishments laid upon them for such crimes.

In both cases, that makes these technologies, and all things built upon them, “Bullshit”, which should be unsurprising given that the technology itself has been most accurately described as “Bullshit Generators”. (see the “ChatGPT is Bullshit” research paper)

The continued existence of these jobs is cemented in place by the threat of starvation and homelessness (aka, the absence of Universal Basic Income, UBI), while blocking concepts like UBI with massive and pointless wasting of resources.

These also cause huge masses of bored and pointless employees to engage ever-more addictively and obsessively with platforms like Twitter, TikTok, and other methods of general time-wasting distraction. These, of course, are also the key consumers of “AI Slop” and major sources of ad-revenue, bored people whose jobs have no reason to exist, and which contribute either virtually or absolutely nothing (positive) to society.
