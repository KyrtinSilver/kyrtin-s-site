One of the reasons why the world’s financial industry seems to love “GenAI” may be a matter of pure similarity. Both are systems explicitly designed not to be understood. Both are also absolutely full of “Bullshit” (the technical term), under the hood.

In David Graeber’s book “Debt: The First 5,000 years”, a quote from Charles Binderup (1937, paraphrased from Henry Ford in 1922) given in the US House of Representatives aptly summarized the situation:

“It is perhaps well enough that the people of the nation do not know or understand our banking and monetary system, for if they did I believe there would be a revolution before tomorrow morning”

The reason for this choice of system has perhaps been most poorly understood (or rather, severely misunderstood) by economists themselves. However, the answer was stumbled upon within the research studies of various Behavioral Economists in recent times, when they discovered that:

• Humans behave progressively more like the amoral greedy maximizers of classical (debunked) economic theory as an increasing number of layers of complexity are inserted into choices and systems.

• The humans in question don’t appear to realize when this change takes place, nor do they reflect on the consequences of their actions or take responsibility for them.

The first major research study in question replaced direct payment for participation with tokens, which were promptly redeemed for cash at another desk within the same area. That rationally minuscule difference, a single added step with zero ambiguity and uncertainty, was sufficient to increase dishonesty.

Looking back to the modern finance industry, it then becomes entirely unsurprising that a countless barrage of completely opaque and indecipherable nonsense is layered upon every system and interaction, seeking to maximize dishonesty and monetary extraction potential.

In Henry Ford’s time it was doubtless far simpler, but today there is never “a revolution before tomorrow morning” because the system was built to not be understood, that is very much by design. One of the most robust mechanisms of control is to hijack the laziness and cognitive limitations of an entire population, making systems so complicated that nobody may be bothered to understand them.

Of course, Graeber’s book “Bullshit Jobs” also aptly highlighted cases such as the 80% of employees at one major bank (48,000 people) whose jobs were entirely pointless, and could be automated away and vanish tomorrow. The reason that the bank didn’t take such action is also fairly simple, not because it cared about said employees, but because all of those extra layers of Bullshit and obfuscation serve a distinct purpose.

As he notes in the book Debt, even the US dollar and overall monetary system of today itself is largely just a form of imperial “tribute” to the US, a tribute paid due to the terrorism that the US exerts via some 800 global military bases. Those protesting against “Kings” obviously haven’t been paying much attention to reality for some time, as this is nothing new.
