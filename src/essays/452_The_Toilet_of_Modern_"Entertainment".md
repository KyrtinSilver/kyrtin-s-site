One of the silver linings of “entertainment” industries, including things like video games and TV shows, going down the toilet en-masse over the past decade or more, is that it makes reading more books that much more appealing by comparison. This downward spiral can be quantified and/or qualified with system dynamics, including:

Dynamic > Cumulative versus Zero-Sum:

-   Scientific Research is an example of Cumulative activity, “standing on the shoulders of giants” by building on the success of others. The exact opposite is true in video games, where one game comes up with an amazing mechanic, then explicitly attempts to prevent others from doing the same, as they play a Zero-Sum game.  
      
    Over time this difference in dynamics causes scientific research to advance and improve, while the quality of games steadily degenerates, unable to integrate the prior successes of others into subsequent works.
    

Dynamic > Complex and Constructive versus KPIs:

-   Again, scientific research, and indeed much of engineering more broadly, is based on systems that are both complex and constructed. This allows for virtually all of the modern wonders of technology that humanity enjoys, such as physical logistics, data transfer and encryption systems, and so on.  
      
    In contrast, imagine if each of those complex and constructed systems were flattened into KPI-based narrow algorithms. Such systems may take the best off-of-the-shelf option available, but they don’t create anything genuinely new, leaving them to ferment if they aren’t supplied with external sources of innovation.
    

Dynamic > Critical Design versus Trope-based:

-   Cognitive Biases are an ever-present temptation, and within games and movies “Tropes” offer a narrative-form library of common, familiar, and generally very boring mechanisms to call upon.  
      
    Research and engineering move forward through applying cognitive labor to the task of critical analysis, design, and experimentation, but various forms of “entertainment” often just toss Tropes into a contextual blender, throw the slop at the wall, and see what sticks. This trope-based slop has only grown in volume with the cognitive offloading to “GenAI”.
    

Much as eating less sugar makes your taste buds more sensitive to subtle flavors, cutting the toilet bowl “entertainment” out of your diet can reliably improve your taste, as well as improving your cognition if you replace that time with reading more research.
