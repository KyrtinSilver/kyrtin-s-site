Just as intelligence requires opportunities to be applied in full, the same is true of the other end of the spectrum, stupidity. The opportunity to be stupid, or “Stupportunity”, is something that methods such as marketing, dark patterns, and the many other exploitations of cognitive biases attempt to maximize.

They do this because a population that is both stupid and predictable, with greatly increased predictability due to the systematic structures imposed by such bad actors, is an easily farmed and maximally exploitable resource. However, this also makes those bad actors both visible and largely predictable themselves, as systematic exploitation not only increases the predictability of others, but it is itself increasingly predictable.

Even accepting the intelligence or absence thereof demonstrated by humans, without consideration for how such intelligence might be improved, there is still every reason to permanently halt and prevent future incarnations of such systems of exploitation, where stupidity is currently maximized among vulnerable populations. This is also relatively easy to accomplish, as global undertakings go, but it does require a lot of on-the-fly adaptation.

One of the key vulnerabilities in systems designed to maximize human “Stupportunity” is that they crank up the unthinking human reliance on such systems, often utilizing the dopamine hits elicited by dark patterns, and false belief patterns about how the world works, who holds “power” and “authority”, and so on. These artificially inflated senses of need, urgency, and related narratives can play into the systems of exploitation that large stupid populations have been herded into, but they may just as easily be used against such malevolent purposes, motivating an exodus.

Once such an exodus is triggered in a given domain, an option that satisfies some want or need, while also minimizing the “Stupportunity” of those gravitating toward it, may be offered, and some gradually increasing number of people may take it. So long as those who take it remain highly positive in this choice, more are likely to follow, even if the original bad actors attempt such methods as slander, general disinformation, or other forms of controversy.

The dynamics in this are similar to matters of Cybersecurity, in that systems where “Stupportunity” has been maximized for exploitative purposes render their users cognitively insecure by design, and that same insecurity may be used against the bad actors who caused and maximized it.

There are plenty of people who don’t regard themselves as intelligent, at least a subset of whom genuinely aren’t, but many such people still contribute to society in highly meaningful ways, supporting those who are. In doing so, many such people prove themselves far more Ethical than some of their more intelligent counterparts.

Indeed, this mirrors some biological systems, such as the glial cells within the human brain, without whom the various types of neurons within the brain couldn’t function nearly as well, if they could function at all. Glial cells have also been shown to engage in similar activity to neurons, though not as quickly, meaning that besides a supporting role, they may contribute more directly, albeit more sparsely.

Just as humans require the opportunity to effectively be “Intelligent” or “Stupid”, part of that opportunity involves having the necessary support, and a big part of that support for the most intelligent has historically come from those far less intelligent, which was itself a statistical inevitability. Not everyone needs to be the “lead character” in some narrative, and many of those who play supporting roles prove themselves far more Ethical than those seeking to lead.

Only a tiny subset of humans are actually suited for Ethical leadership of any kind, with most of that subset being systematically denied such opportunities in the modern world, as a bad actor’s best defense is to never let them into the same building, barring the gates against any competence that out-competes their malevolence. Such practices have grown into entire industries and bureaucratic institutions of their own, but the increasingly extreme nature of such endeavors also makes them increasingly fragile and vulnerable from all other angles.

All of these structures of malevolence are little more than sand castles, waiting to be washed away with the tide. Economic Bubbles couldn’t emerge in the familiar ways without this manner of Stupportunity exploitation, and once the current AI Bubble has been popped, the opportunity to prevent future bubbles may be reinforced by the widespread motivation to do so.
