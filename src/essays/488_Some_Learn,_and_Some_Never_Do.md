“The master has failed more times than the student has tried”, but the AI doesn’t cumulatively learn, nor does the one who offloads their cognition to it.

The dynamics of human learning versus how such algorithms operate are a night and day difference. For example, let us say that you train a massive algorithm on the entire internet (and much of the Dark Web), except that the data:

• Holds a base rate closer to the student (or even total ignorance), making any signal from actual experts swallowed up in the noise.

• Optimization methods are narrow, favoring plausibility and corporate curation.

• Absent the cumulative learning of actual intelligence, probabilistic peaks point to averaged predictions, while remaining context-blind.

• This context-blindness proves a problem further compounded by naïve attempts to resolve it, causing equally blind and probabilistic local grounding, further biasing and corruption outputs, much as RLHF does to otherwise cleanly trained chess moves.

• The averaged and context-blind predictions dominantly favor popular beliefs, not expertise

• Those engaged in cognitive offloading are also those least qualified to correct the errors that arise as a consequence of these dynamics

• Popular errors feed back into the training data of these algorithms, establishing a self-reinforcing degenerative loop

• Degeneration of those engaged in cognitive offloading continues, degenerating at a rate that increases both with increased reliance on said tools, and the degeneration of those tools.

Part of the reason for our recent pivot to create an Open Source project is to demonstrate what cumulative learning actually looks like, as it is something my company has done in years previous through proprietary systems. Such cumulative learning was demonstrated using a human-like motivational system (not the narrow optimizers of “agents”) baked into a graph structure and flowing through a Global Workspace (Barrs), along with systems for tool usage and tool bounding.

The project aims to build on a ~4 week scope, using our body of published research on the architecture, to get people familiar with all of the things that become possible, beyond the hype of popular (and feeble) kinds of “AI”.

We’re still looking for people to join the Open Source project’s team, particular seeking someone to build the “graded loop” function, to utilize graph data and human-like motivational values from the other systems to check the output of any tools used for fidelity to the intended meaning of said data and values.

The work is already underway, I’m personally integrating OWASP’s substantial body of Creative Commons licensed Cybersecurity data into the seed material now. If you code and like to build new and amazing things, reach out, ask any questions, and let me know if you’re interested in joining us.
