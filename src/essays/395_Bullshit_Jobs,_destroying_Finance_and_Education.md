By the numbers, 37-50% of jobs (before GenAI) were “Bullshit Jobs”, even by the self-assessment of those working them. This means that they were the subset of people in actual Bullshit Jobs who couldn’t rationalize their way out of this realization, and who drank no Kool-Aid to prevent such.

In finance, this problem was found to be significantly worse, with 48,000 out of 60,000 employees (80%) for one major bank being utterly pointless, to the degree where they could be removed and automated (over 7 years ago) without anyone noticing.

Part of what cements these jobs in place is the fear of unemployment, and all the terrible things associated with it. Likewise, even partially Bullshit Jobs, where endless paperwork rains from the toilets on high, are effectively prevented from adapting by this dynamic.

Predictably, leaving such wastes of time unchecked allows them to fester and ferment, growing worse over time. This problem is heavily documented for US universities in particular, directly causing astronomical increases in tuition, even as the value of attending those same universities has virtually collapsed with free and open access to educational materials.

Another problem that may be traced back to this extreme rise in administrative staff (along with 300% tuition increases) is that of “Cancel Culture” as it emerged on such campuses, and was cultivated by those in Bullshit Jobs. This topic is covered at some length in the research of both Jonathan Haidt (in “The Coddling of the American Mind”) and the subsequent work of Greg Lukianoff (in “The Canceling of the American Mind”).

Another telling example noted in Bullshit Jobs was a quote from Barack Obama stating that it could be significantly more efficient and effective to avoid private Health Insurance for the US’s plans that his name came to be stamped on. His excuse?

“Everybody who supports single-payer health care says, ‘Look at all this money we would be saving from insurance and paperwork,’ ” the former President noted. “That represents one million, two million, three million jobs.”

Even when government recognizes these problems, they often do nothing to address them. Rather, they enshrine these problems in layers of bureaucracy, to prevent them from being remedied. As of this writing, that same noose now appears set to hang some 20 million US citizens, in exchange for 1, 2, or 3 million Bullshit Jobs.

I and others in tech hubs have also observed a steady rise in the number of people in prestigious IT, software, and consulting firms who find what they do utterly meaningless, including Google, Microsoft, Amazon, SpaceX, Meta, and major consultancies. That objectively terrible 2-year expected turnover rate in the tech industry isn’t just for higher pay, it is also a search for meaning (often never fulfilled).
