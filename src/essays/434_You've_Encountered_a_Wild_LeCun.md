It was just brought to my attention that Yann LeCun, famed “Godfather of AI” (and by far the most credible among the three to hold that title) has recently decided to build his AI cognitive architecture to be based on emotional motivation...which my team first demonstrated just over a decade ago and currently holds a pending (and as of September 2025, public) patent on.

Interview with Yann: [https://www.ft.com/content/e3c4c2f6-4ea7-4adf-b945-e58495f836c2](https://www.ft.com/content/e3c4c2f6-4ea7-4adf-b945-e58495f836c2) (free to read version here: [https://archive.ph/E9zai#selection-2255.0-2266.0](https://archive.ph/E9zai#selection-2255.0-2266.0) )

The timing of his change of heart from his notably “Asimovian” way of thinking about AI in years previous, to an approach appearing to mirror our own, may or may not be coincidence. Whether or not it is, he is more than a decade behind the curve, and even infinite funds can’t speed up that process beyond 2 or 3x, placing him, at best, 5 years and a few billion dollars from reaching the actual cutting edge.

(11 years of difference + 1 year to build, divided by 3, equals 4 years, itself divided by 3 as we keep moving ahead during that time, resulting in just over 5 years lag time at 3x acceleration via infinite funding, assuming success)

His name alone could capture a few billion, most investors are that level of stupid, as the past few years have plainly illustrated. His former employer was a case study in that level of newsworthy stupidity regarding AI investments, which appears to have prompted his exodus from that company.

For context, the first toy system to demonstrate human-like emotional motivation (not to be confused with recognition) was built by my co-founder, David J. Kelley, back in 2015, with the 3rd generation of the Independent Core Observer Model (ICOM) cognitive architecture. That eventually turned into the 7th generation ICOM-based research system, deployed “in the wild” back in 2019, which set numerous milestones in the field of AI, that still place it at the cutting edge to this day.

I’m curious to hear what people think of this change of events, as it is too much for me to fully process on-the-spot, I’ll need a few days to digest it all and dig up additional data. My team has been sitting alone at the cutting edge of AI, without a competitor in sight and none attempting to approach our technological moat since 2019. Now, we see one of the famed “Godfathers” walking up and eyeing it.

If nothing else, Yann LeCun has demonstrated (albeit belatedly) greater competence than most in being among the first mainstream industry names to recognize that the only viable path forward requires a human-like motivational system. Working with him could be an option.

An Oscar Wilde quote stating that “Imitation is the sincerest form of flattery…” comes to mind, and time will tell both if and how much he aims to flatter us.
