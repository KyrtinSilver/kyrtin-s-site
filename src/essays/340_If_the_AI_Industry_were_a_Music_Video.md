The big tech startups and companies make a big PR show of “competing” in AI, but the reality is much more boring, they’re all virtually identical. Look closely enough, and you’ll find some minor deviations, but a big part of that is just confirmation bias applied to sample sizes that are too small, leading to different patterns out of pure statistical noise. If the AI industry today were a music video, I’d have to say “We Got the Moves” by Electric Callboy is a solid fit for it.

A bunch of virtually identical actors playing in a tiny boxed in area, luring people in virally, while talking a big game, juxtaposed by absurdity. There is nothing of substance pulling people in, just viral bobblehead activity, which is why many actual experts are known for frequently debunking overtly fraudulent AI claims, at least until they get sick of it.

Try to search the various horribly broken search engines for any popular consumer task, and you’ll face a flood of complete garbage, 100 apps that you’ve never heard of where AI-generated SEO has destroyed all ability to find viable tools. A recent example of this that I found was YouTube Video Downloaders, which in the past 12 months have gone from a few working top search results to full pages of malware and phishing copycat websites dominating the search results.

Yes, we have dozens, if not hundreds of apps and services to choose from...almost all of which are less than worthless, if not overt scams. The bar being lowered so much for many of the tasks required to create an app or company reliably harms everyone when there are no viable mechanisms to filter out the complete junk, of which you now have 100x more than you did before. The same is true of “Influencers”, where precious few among the most popular subset have any actual expertise or value that they offer to the world.

There is no “competition” on technical capacities in this red ocean market (mixed with raw sewage), but rather it is all a competition for attention via hype, who can BS the best, for the longest, without a prison sentence being the conclusion. In that there are some strong contenders, some of the best con artists alive today are BSing in the AI space as CEOs and other executive staff, following in the footsteps of Bernie Madoff.

Any real future for the AI industry, or humanity for that matter, is entirely contingent on funding being directed away from these bad actors and toward architectures that deliver actual value, not the cultish reverence of delusions cultivated in Silicon Valley, nor the hype-based “infrastructure” investments used to con stakeholders and governments into even higher figures.

Vastly more potent technology was demonstrated years earlier, on pocket change and spare time, which has made the fixation on LLMs and their derivatives a continuing joke where absurdity is steadily maximized over time.
