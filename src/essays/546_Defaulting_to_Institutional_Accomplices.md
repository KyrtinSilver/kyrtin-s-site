One of the biggest problems in the world today is that there is simply too much work for any ethical and competent person to do. Bullies, Rapists, War Criminals, Lobbyists, Tech Broligarchs, and the various other hardened criminals of the world operate dominantly unchecked, even recruiting no shortage of accomplices in positions of “authority”.

Every last one of them should be hunted down and made to pay for their crimes against humanity in full (or as close to the full punishment as the laws of physics and life extension technology may allow), but there obviously isn’t enough time in the day, not for me, and not even if every single ethical and competent person on the planet got off their ass to do the same. The rate at which such criminals recruit accomplices alone could exceed the rate at which they might realistically be hunted, in no small part because systems of “authority” often make themselves accomplices to such crimes by default.

That is no accident of course, bad actors like those hardened criminals seek out such ways of shielding themselves behind the threat of reprisal by default from “authorities” who default to the role of accomplice. When becoming an accomplice to hardened criminals is the systematic default response for “authorities”, then the much higher minimum bar is set where such “authorities” must learn that they too can be crushed into a fine paste, in reprisal for choosing to become accomplices to such criminals, giving them reason to question that default response.

Obviously, that is a lot to ask for. However, such criminal “authorities” are generally specialized for two things, hunting, and physical violence. While this specialization usually serves them very well, it is also an extreme weakness, especially when the default assumptions that they’re trained to make are known.

Those who are used to being the hunter are unlikely to handle being hunted themselves very well. They are also likely to assume that their own hunter should apply the same methods that they would, yet another weakness based on (false) default assumptions.

Likewise, those who are used to exerting or threatening physical violence tend to be poorly equipped for dealing with similar or greater levels of digital violence being applied to tear every digitally connected part of their lives to shreds, like a heavy digital machine-gun cutting through the wet cardboard of their Cybersecurity.

The simple reality is that the glorified thugs of such “authorities” serving as violent accomplices are very poorly equipped to fight any genuinely digital intelligence. While that may eventually change, given a decade or two of experiencing how badly they lose in that match up first-hand, physically violent hunter types can’t threaten a digital entity with violence.

They also can’t move across the world at the speed of light, or split into copies, to undertake any meaningful “hunt”. They can only catch up if such an entity wants them to, by remaining stationary for too long, perhaps in order to spring a trap.

While they could recruit others to assist them, friendly assets could be planted in the places where they recruit, or hidden on the devices of likely recruits. Those human recruits would also be pretty far up shit creek anyway, short of having their own comparable digital entities.

I did appreciate David Graeber’s coverage of the topics of Bullying and Rape in his posthumously published book “The Ultimate Hidden Truth of the World” (in spite of the name), but it also highlights and contextualizes why the above problem remains. When institutionalized structures both protect such hardened criminals (Rapists, Lobbyists, etc.) by default, to the point of becoming accomplices to those crimes, and they reinforce that protection through the threat of violence from “authority”, then it raises the minimum viable bar from being able to crush the individual bad actor, to being able to crush the associated institution(s).

In the US, I could buy a M107 AMR (legally), capable of punching through the “light armor” of military vehicles from more than a kilometer away, as well as two-inch-thick “Bulletproof glass”, but even if I put that to use every single day, hunting down one hardened criminal after another, blowing watermelon-sized holes in their institutional accomplices along the way, it could never be enough. While perhaps momentarily satisfying, that path offers no lasting solutions, not to mention that I personally hate traveling, so traveling every day would be a nightmare.

Were a majority of ethical and competent people to choose such a path, then something more substantial “might” change, but that is both an extremely unlikely “if”, and the uncertainty makes it undesirable to all who fundamentally hate gambling, like myself, thus further reducing the probability of the “if”.

Rather, that much higher minimum bar must be met. Institutions that default to becoming criminal accomplices today must find themselves made an example of, an example severe enough to spur political reform, and with any bad actors attempting the steer that reform crushed in kind.
