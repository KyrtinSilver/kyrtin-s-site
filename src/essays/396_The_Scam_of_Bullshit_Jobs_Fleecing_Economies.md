How can we even begin to solve the problem of “Bullshit Jobs”?

Employees, companies, and even governments already frequently recognize the problem, yet they continue choosing to do nothing about it, even encouraging it to grow worse from one year to the next. The problem generally isn’t awareness.

The incentives, both positive and negative, that support this “managerial feudalism”, as Graeber termed it, must be up-ended. Often the risk of “Free Riders” (truly parasitic individuals) are noted as an argument against concepts like Universal Basic Income (UBI). There are “provisionally valid” arguments against UBI (valid absent the correct technology being applied), but that isn’t one of them.

By the numbers, the typical ratio of genuine bad actors to everyone else is often around 1:7500 (Software Developer figures), meaning that only ~0.013% will likely still gravitate toward malevolent purposes of their own accord, all else being equal.

Even if such bad actors were left entirely unchecked and untouched, which absolutely nothing indicates that we’d need to settle for, then they’d still be that tiny fraction of a percent, versus 50%+ of the population currently working in utterly pointless jobs that exert a net-negative influence on society, as well as the individuals themselves.

To put this into more Game Theoretic terms:

Option A: Pay $1,000 to avoid a fee of $0.26, with a 5% annual markup on the amount to be paid.

Option B: Pay $1,000 for $1,000 worth of actual goods and services, and with no creeping markup.

The rational option is, of course, extremely obvious.

The reality of the problem is also demonstrably worse in monetary terms, as there is the strong observed correlation between higher income jobs being more worthless, and even detrimental, to the rest of society, meaning that the ~50% of Bullshit Jobs might be soaking up 80%+ of the actual income, which could push the above example to something more like “Pay $2,600 to avoid a fee of $0.26”.

Both the problem and absence of solutions being deployed, such as UBI, are gated and otherwise restricted by the hyper-complexity of modern society, and fundamental limits on how well purely human groups may address these problems. Many Bullshit Jobs are allegedly there to “increase efficiency”, even when they demonstrably do the opposite, but one reason that they predictably fail is because they were never designated to handle hyper-complexity in the first place.

That is why my team has focused for more than a decade on building systems that are fundamentally capable of dealing with hyper-complexity, across any and all domains, in the untouched “Blue Ocean Market” of often global (and severe) problems impacting virtually all sectors, due to levels of complexity that no human, group of humans, or “AI tools” may even begin to handle. They are the elephant in every room, the turd on every plate, and the domains where the most money flows are often among those most impacted by it today.

Across all scenarios where human extinction is avoided, Bullshit Jobs will become a historical footnote, one way or another. People today will choose if they become a part of that future, or not.
