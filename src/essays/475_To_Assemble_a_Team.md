I must admit that I’m starting to get a bit excited for the project (which I suppose will need a name), as my calendar is starting to fill up with virtual coffees to meet with everyone who could not just make it happen, but also potentially add more to it than I’d initially taken aim at.

Initially the concept was to start with applying the published work on the Independent Core Observer Model (ICOM) cognitive architecture’s motivational system, tied to a schema-less graph structure, utilized via a kind of “Global Workspace”. That involves the motivational system baked into a schema-less graph structure, allowing it to be dynamically navigated with contextual sensitivity, shifting priorities, and dynamic expansion along arbitrary dimensions and subject matter.

With the possible addition of a formal mathematician with Chaos Theory experience and someone with their own Hypergraph architecture, then more interesting elements of Chaos Theory may be applied to graph structure and processing to take all of this several steps further.

There are also some significant opportunities on the Cybersecurity side, such as potentially baking multi-encryption into a dynamic graph structure, which to my knowledge hasn’t been done before. If you know a skilled cryptographer with multi-encryption experience, I’ll be adding that to my team Wishlist for phase 2 of this.

For reference material on the published portions of ICOM, as related to motivation, see:

Non-Logical Simulation Model-based Decision-making Systems to Drive Self-Motivation in Software Systems: [https://doi.org/10.13140/RG.2.2.21015.38565](https://doi.org/10.13140/RG.2.2.21015.38565)

Modeling Emotions in a Computational System - Emotional Modeling in the Independent Core Observer Model Cognitive Architecture: [https://www.researchgate.net/publication/303445396\_Modeling\_Emotions\_in\_a\_Computational\_System\_-\_Emotional\_Modeling\_in\_the\_Independent\_Core\_Observer\_Model\_Cognitive\_Architecture](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/303445396_Modeling_Emotions_in_a_Computational_System_-_Emotional_Modeling_in_the_Independent_Core_Observer_Model_Cognitive_Architecture)

As loath as I am to (indirectly) feed the hype train of fundamentally non-viable “GenAI”, what temporary gains that bump in hype may offer them, it is nothing compared to what may quickly be gained from driving interest and funding into viable technologies.

Better yet, this project can help people to wrap their heads around some basic concepts that most in the AI field have overlooked or neglected, allowing for both rapid learning and hands-on iteration within Open Source.

People can begin to understand the benefits of a schema-less graph structure, able to model the real world. They can observe and test how various motivational system configurations navigate across data landscapes of arbitrary dimensionality. They can find all manner of new tricks and tweaks to improve stability, compute optimization, and performance metrics. They can begin to learn how the seeds of such systems are created, how each type of data and ordering factor into it, and how those seeds grow differently depending on circumstances and variables, as each graph iteratively expands and refines.

I’m ready to pop that bubble of DegenerativeAI. We can, and should, do better.
