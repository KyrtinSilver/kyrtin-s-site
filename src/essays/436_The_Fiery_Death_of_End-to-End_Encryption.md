What does an “Agentic OS” mean?

“All of your data, all of the time, as much as possible” - Meredith Whittaker of Signal

For a full brief on the Cybersecurity hazards of this, the 39th Chaos Communication Congress (39C3) just dropped an excellent presentation from Signal’s staff, people who’re deeply familiar with these Cybersecurity problems: [39C3 - AI Agent, AI Spy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ANECpNdt-4)

By embedding surveillance into the operating system, it negates the very purpose of End-to-End Encryption (E2EE), by allowing the operating system to create a honeypot of your most sensitive information.

Their coverage of MCP vulnerabilities in the video actually understates the problem, as the MCPLIB Attack Framework covers a much wider range of devastating vulnerabilities in MCP.

Malicious code and content can be triggered by such systems without even being accessed, such as the “Summarize unread emails” command that they note, or via malicious tools that are never accessed, but which modify the usage of other tools.

They also address some fundamental math that both VCs and ordinary people need to familiarize themselves with. “Agentic” (BS) is based on multi-step performance, but probabilistic systems with even 95% accuracy per-step decay to ~21.4% accuracy by step 30.

95% per-step accuracy in the real world for such systems is itself also a pipe-dream in most cases, where they’ve been shown to perform at 30% accuracy (for the best models), with 34-94% “Hallucination” (aka Bullshit) rates exceeding that even on simple 1-step search queries.

You also get absolute absurdity out of trying this, like an “agent” failing to find an employee, then renaming another employee to match the query.

These LLM-based systems are peddled to gullible suckers as though they “serve” the user, but the user with an “Agentic OS” owns nothing, has no privacy, and is always under surveillance. They are a slave, and an exceptionally stupid one, as they chose to become a slave.

Now here is the quiet part that I’ll say out loud:

There is not a single country on the face of the planet that will have the faintest shred of secret or confidential information, privacy for their citizens or internal government operations, or any capacities derivative thereof. The same is true of larger companies.  
  
If such systems are baked into OS’s by default, even if they are turned off by default, not only will they be vulnerable-by-design, they’ll make every single system that they hold privileged access to vulnerable-by-design as well. That is the overwhelming majority of the world’s hardware, all vulnerable, all at once.

An “agent”, by definition, acts without your consent.
