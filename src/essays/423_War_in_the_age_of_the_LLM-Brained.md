While the relative economic incentives of war are worth noting, the ideological factors tend to weigh more heavily in any violent engagement, be it at the scale of individuals or nations. As the ideologies proliferating across the world are increasingly isolated and fermented into more extreme versions via “AI” echo chambers and 1-on-1 sycophantic mirroring, we can expect to see a steadily increasing number of casualties, such as those of the recent mass-shooting at Bondi Beach in Australia.

Emotions are known to be the human motivational system for individuals, as the research literature has thoroughly covered, but at the scale of groups and nations such motivations are systematized and structured into what we call “ideologies”, shared motivational frameworks. Like emotions, these have no inherent relationship with reality or rationality, despite being systematically structured. They can be just as unhinged as any individual’s motives.

Such ideologies mix about as well with LLM-based technology as illicit drugs do with alcohol, producing “AI Psychosis” with steadily increasing regularity. You also can’t regulate ideologies, or even realistically limit their spread, making them substantially more dangerous than literal viruses in practice. While plenty of people have feared novel viral bioweapons for decades, they should probably fear pathogenic ideologies more today.

Ideologies are reliably “useful, but wrong” tools for the cognitive toolbox, cognitive shortcuts to avoid critical thinking and hard higher cognitive labor. They can be valuable, but they can also lead entire populations into absurd wars and terrorism of all kinds. This is globally visible in how no less than 5 countries have operated for decades, China, Russia, the US, Israel, and Türkiye, to name a few. All operate with a heavy emphasis on terrorizing foreign populations in various ways, through threats, occupations, bombing, and even invasions.

Ideologies have made this possible, polished and refined to serve as tools to render local populations docile against such activity. However, that also can’t withstand the current rapid fermentation process of AI, particularly as it is also a rapid “fragmentation” process, due to the 1-on-1 sycophancy lending itself to divergence, even under various feeble corporate attempts at “guardrailing” (fraudrailing).

A big part of this traces back to the root problem of complexity, as each fragmentation causes new combinatorial explosions in the required complexity to cope with that change. Governments today can’t handle the complexity that they’ve had to deal with for the past 10 years, let alone the new and rapidly growing levels that they’re being hit with. They can run from it, throwing all of the tools in the old toolbox at it, but beyond that they can only “go for broke” and gamble on whatever technology and methods remain on the table.

That leads to the next predictable and measurably worse outcome that we can expect. “Degenerative AI” is being positioned to serve as that “go for broke” option, potentially the final nail in the coffin for many governments. This also isn’t occurring in isolation, but as one of many comorbidities, including current and likely near-future wars.

Nothing inspires the mass-delusion of nationalism quite like a shared “enemy”, real or imagined, and LLM-based AI can take that several big steps further into fully unhinged territory.
