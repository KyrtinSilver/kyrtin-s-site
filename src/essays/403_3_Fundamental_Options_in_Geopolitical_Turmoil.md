In any given serious geopolitical environment, you have at least 3 fundamental options. This also holds true in virtually any case where you’re given a binary choice, as binary choices tend to be a lie of omission, if not worse. For example, let’s look to history, nearly a century ago in the case of Nazi Germany:

1) Many left the country, recognizing that a hostile force had (or would) seized control of their government and military forces.  
  
“Here are brewing economically and politically dark times, so I’m happy to be able to get away from everything.” – Albert Einstein, August 1922  

  
  
In the case of Einstein, he wrote this a full year before the Nazis even attempted their first (failed) coup. We don’t all need to be visionaries with such foresight, as baseline “sight” is now all that is required to observe the same patterns in certain countries today.

2) Many stayed, working against that hostile government from within Germany and the newly invaded countries surrounding it, leading to stories like that of Anne Frank, with great strife, and some (few) survivors.  
  
Alternatively, and many orders of magnitude more effectively, Che Guevara, now a pop-culture icon, was a strong example of someone who genuinely fought (and won) against dictators and hostile invaders, such as the military training which repelled the US’s failed “Bay of Pigs” invasion.

\*In strategy and tactics, guerrilla warfare, is still very relevant and potentially effective today, but the technical skill of the guerrilla must at least match that of the hostile forces.

3) Lastly, there are always collaborators. Some are simply exploiting every opportunity that they’re given, meeting the definition of “Amoral”, or indifferent to moral consideration. Others actually choose to believe whatever narrative they’re being handed by such hostile forces, which for most of a given population is usually the “path of least resistance” at any single step. Considered over many steps, this usually ceases to be the case, but by that time the Cognitive Bias of Sunk Cost Fallacy has already taken root. “Stockholm Syndrome” can also take effect, named after the events of World War II.

In summary, Relocation, Insurrection, and Accomplice are the three fundamental choices in geopolitically extreme environments. The world is a big place, so relocation is frequently an option to many destinations, funds and/or connections allowing. The other two mean remaining in the burning house, either as a firefighter (insurrection), or as a purveyor of gasoline (accomplice).

Some things may only be accomplished from outside, and some only from inside. The two also tend to be complementary, and all dictators and extreme (and violent) regimes eventually fall.

How will history remember you?

Are you an Einstein, one who contributes to the fall of regimes from outside? Are you a Che Guevara, who overthrows such regimes from within? Are you an Anne Frank, who passively resists and hides? Are you a Pierre Daye, collaborating with the regime, and tied to the fate of it?

Who are you?
