An update on the ICOM-based OSS project: 1 of the 4 segments is now completed, the open-source compatible seed. It consists of just over 2,900 nodes, each containing roughly a page worth of contents, and with 45 total contextual connections (aka surfaces) in the graph, 35 specific and 10 abstract. Each node also has a single assigned type, a source, and a full set of emotional valences, as well as interest and need values assigned.

I did have to limit this seed to a stable core subset (philosophy, concepts, direct alignment, etc.) combined with Cybersecurity specific knowledge (OWASP, NIST, hacking, prompt engineering, etc.), leaving a second seed for a later date, due largely to the difficulty of specific licenses on the mainstays of medical knowledge, as well as the added time investment a second seed would entail.

Instead, I’ll be switching to work on a simplified version of the Global Workspace (Baars) that was used in our 7th gen ICOM-based research system, alongside another of our team of volunteer contributors (a formal mathematician). Besides the seed material construction, which only my co-founder and I currently have the depth of knowledge for, building such a simplified Global Workspace is the next most difficult thing to find someone with the specialized expertise to architect and construct.

The OSS project still needs people with the technical skills, time, and motivation to actually contribute code. In particular, the bread and butter SWE skills to deal with databases and parsers, deployments and integrations, as well as the mix of other AI/ML and SWE skills to validate and iterate upon data using tools. Everything is listed on a team Kanban now, but I’ve completed ¼ of the workload myself, without a single bit of code being contributed by anyone else to-date. People have gotten sick, had new jobs to start, and so on, with some excuses being valid.

Personally, I kept working on it while sick, even on a day with a 103 degree Fahrenheit fever, so I take such excuses with a big grain of salt. I don’t hold others up to the same standard that I hold myself to, but there are limits.

I will say that if I end up having to do the majority, or all, of the work on the project myself, as could still end up being the case at this point, then it won’t be open-sourced. If that happens, then the people who actually contributed could benefit from it, to whatever degree that they contributed...and the rest will be a surprise. Either way, there will definitely be demonstrations, just with measurably more panic in the market if I’m deeply pissed off from having to do it all myself.

The project still needs a proper name selected. A few that we’ve tossed around were “My Little Shoggoth”, “ThanOSS”, “The Art of Lore” (Star Trek: TNG reference), or perhaps “Moriarty OSS”. Since the tech industry naming conventions are to name a company or project after the exact opposite concept (“OpenAI”, “Anthropic”, etc.), we thought it would be appropriate to name the project after various villains and vile concepts like Shoggoth.

The lovely thing about building an ethically aligned system is that it absolutely can treat the deeply unethical like a proper Shoggoth, cut their valuation in half like a good Thanos, and demonstrate the illusive performance in Cybersecurity like Moriarty evading Holmes.
