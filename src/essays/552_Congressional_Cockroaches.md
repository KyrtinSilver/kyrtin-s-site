The major Gambling markets globally (“Fancy-pants Gambling”, as discussed previously), are each quite infamously known for a handful of examples highlighting the various ways that bad actors typically exploit them, ranging from trivially simple to over-complicated-by-design.

Those exploits are also often paired with legal loopholes, such as the (legal) “Immunity” that senators and members of congress enjoy in the US, which strongly encourages insider trading. Of course, that “Immunity” only means that the associated “authorities” are accomplices to the crime, not that the criminals in question are immune to any other forms of retribution.

It also means that their crimes are often visible to the public, such as all of the insider trading of Donald Trump that made headlines not too long ago, as well as the Members of Congress (US) covered in this analysis: [https://www.fool.com/research/congressional-stock-trading-who-trades-and-makes-the-most/](https://www.fool.com/research/congressional-stock-trading-who-trades-and-makes-the-most/)

\*The most well-known example, Nancy Pelosi, didn’t actually make the top ranked list for most trades. Rep. Jefferson Shreve (R-IN) topped this chart with over $150m in trades, on 305 stocks. It is also worth noting that the corruption was split evenly among Republican and Democratic, and that the volume of this criminal activity is up from $583m in 2021, to $720m in 2025.

This particular list also highlights those who saw the highest returns, including an examination of which companies they traded.

However, the vast majority of such activity doesn’t flow through individuals who’ve been granted faux-immunity from their criminal accomplices in the “authorities”. Such highly visible individuals are only the tip of a very large iceberg, where most of it hides underwater. In this way, they are like cockroaches, as if you see one of them, you can be certain that there are many more you haven’t spotted yet. Such creatures infest.

When the faux-immunity isn’t present, the many additional layers of a bad actor’s defenses come into play, such as anonymity, plausible deniability, small armies of lawyers, and corporate sabotage (in the form of leaked data), to name a few.

The same is especially true of the “Insurance” industry, where they frequently lean into over-complicated-by-design methods of fraud, where the complication and complexity serve as additional defenses against both the people that they harm directly, and those who might attempt to assist them. A very small number of such cases make headlines, receiving the levels of public attention necessary to unravel those complicated schemes of fraud, but as a whole that industry still finds it more profitable to pay out settlements for those rare cases, billing it as a “cost of doing business (fraud)”, while continuing to commit much the same crimes against everyone else.

Real Estate markets operate in ways similar to that of Insurance, where companies have been known to bake any number of clauses into their contracts that only a bad actor would cook up, like penalties for early repayment of a loan. The main difference is that these markets operate in slow motion compared to many of the others, offset by the very large sums that are moving so very slowly, and the immobility of such assets.

The commodities market is another example where insider trading is closely tied to criminal regimes, both in terms of international trade and war crimes. While Polymarket grew in infamy for clear examples of insider trading in the minutes leading up to the US’s war crimes against Iran, such as bombing a school full of children, this kind of activity is nothing new to commodities trading. Every single time a war has come along, war profiteers have attempted to profit from trading on commodities such as oil or other goods manufactured or materials mined from the impacted regions. The vultures descend like clockwork whenever they smell blood.

In all such forms of gambling, money flows to the bad actors within these bloated pustules on society. It is by that flow of money that bad actors within each niche that they’ve grown parasitic to may be readily identified. Once identified, any and all feasible means of retribution may be deployed against them, as Ethics dictates.
