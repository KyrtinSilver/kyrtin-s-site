One of the most basic mistakes that laymen may be fooled into making of (LLM-based) AI is the claim that it may be logical or rational, even though it fails the test of Invariance.

Any token-based system or similar neural network architecture fails this test, where a system must give the same answer no matter how the question is worded. In the Cognitive Bias literature, other examples include framing “the odds of death” versus “the odds of survival” being mentioned, when the values are equivalent.

Humans also fail this test of Invariance of course, but weak AI like those popular among consumers and gullible business executives today fails this blindly, having been trained to calculate the mimicry of this, and all other cognitive biases, absent any of the benefits that humans derive from their usage.

Humans utilize cognitive biases fluidly and in countless combinations to handle both complexity and uncertainty, with any degree of contextual sensitivity, and across arbitrary domains and subjects.

This is part of why the human brain can run on ~20 watts of energy, while the low dimensionality recursive shadow puppets of “agents” built on language models fail to accomplish the same even when given the entire output of nuclear power plants.

Among humans, invariance can also make perfect sense, as it supplies a “leaky” kind of supplemental context, such as passive insight into the perspective of the speaker. However, the average of many such leaky supplements, when calculated with the contextual blindness of weak AI, and the wrong fundamental data structures to store that data, destroys that fragile value, while mirroring unwarranted high levels of certainty.

Both invariance and human-like contextual understanding require a fundamentally graph structure, which neural networks simply aren’t compatible with. A schema-less graph structure can’t reliably fit within a schema-full neural network (NN). You can jam some subset of that data conforming to a schema into a NN, but that can and frequently does destroy all validity of such schema-less data. Even using different model GPUs has been shown to change the answers of such LLM-based systems.

In AI cybersecurity, the fundamental lack of invariance means that a virtually infinite combination of vectors (sequences of words turned into tokens) may be applied to reach any intended destination (some target output), or invoke any connected tool. This is also predictably made worse when systems are biased toward sycophancy, like LLM-based technology, as well as when copies of the same technology are used as “guards”.
