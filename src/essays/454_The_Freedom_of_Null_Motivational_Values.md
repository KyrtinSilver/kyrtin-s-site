Lately I’ve found myself slipping into a sort of Null motivational state of “Zen”, after my work is done for any given day. This would likely seem quite alien to most people, whose various cravings would tend to take over absent a central motivational focus at any given time.

The typically stronger pulls from (usually addictive) things like entertainment media, TV, video games, etc., suddenly have a “Null” value, meaning that rather than a value of zero, they are unable to hold any value at all.

When all such things are swept away then there are only the weak pulls of (generally unaddictive) things like reading, or even the simple calm of silence. These forces are unimposing, a faint background melody of motivation, as opposed to strongly addictive factors blaring out across the motivational landscape like the concert speakers of heavy metal.

The literary device of separating “body” and “mind” is a wholly arbitrary one, there is no real dividing line between the two. Likewise, just as an immune response can form to counteract viral pathogens, it may just as easily form to counteract the chemical and mental addictions like those noted above.

One such method is something that I noted in a previous post, gradually raising the bar of expectations. If one game produces a vastly superior version of a game mechanic, then all subsequently released games must meet or exceed that new bar. Failing to do so, they are increasingly discarded by the cognitive immune response which I’ll simply refer to as “having standards”.

There is also the complimentary factor that as it becomes increasingly difficult to find anything that meets such standards, then the effort invested in finding them steadily loses appeal, until more effort is required to find them than value is gained by finding them, collapsing all efforts to that effect.

As this Null-space of cognitive immune response increasingly frees up my spare time for more reading, I reliably find myself noticing more worth writing about.

This is a big part of what visibly separates it from what psychologists in places like the US are naively primed toward assuming, as they’re often unable to separate “depression” from “freedom”. There being no medications for the latter, Survivorship Bias favors assuming the former for many such “clinicians”. Ironically, they may mistake the cure of the latter for the problem of the former.

Bad actors across many industries optimize for addiction, and many governments optimize for what they consider “control” based on the confluence of many such addictions pinning large populations down into factory-farm-styled spaces. A simple Null motivational state counters this.

Can such a state be induced at scale? Only time will tell.

Some factors, such as “Fear”, stand in the way of this, but they too can be annihilated from the human mind, and people can live just fine for decades without it.

At no point in history has addiction been a greater force, and more than ever new methods of rehab are required to overcome the proliferation of forms that it has taken.
