“Enabling people” to produce copious amounts of c\*&p is a Tragedy of the Commons that ultimately harms not only those being “enabled”, but all people.

At the same time, a flood of sub-mediocrity creates several very real, profitable, and deeply malevolent “business opportunities”, tied to controlling what small subset of that flood any given individual or group may be exposed to.

On Social Media platforms, even those masquerading as “business-oriented”, exposing target Group A to algorithmically “personalized” post subset B is the industry standard method of cultivating both extremism and increasingly lucrative echo chambers, where “engagement” is maximized at any cost, and that cost is itself a “sold commodity” for political bad actors who stand to benefit from polarization and extremists (both for and against them).

The malevolent platform thus extracts direct ad-revenue, a subset of which comes directly from scams, as well as additional revenue from cultivating “targeted” echo chambers for political, religious, or other cult-like extremist purposes. Worse yet, as these delusions are cultivated some people are even foolish enough to ACTUALLY PAY FOR this kind of treatment, with the last vestiges of any competence that might have otherwise been usually dispersing the moment that a “Premium” membership was paid for. That last one isn’t a hard rule, but it is true the overwhelming majority of the time.

On other platforms with this problem of sub-mediocrity content floods, the resulting damage can take a wider variety of forms. In any space where there are a finite number of product names that may be selected, and once selected they may not be taken again, then such floods cause a rapid and potentially permanent deterioration in the quality of names. This functions similar to the dot-com bubble, except that these names lose value more like a car being driven off of the sales lot (or a cliff), and as naming becomes an increasingly dire s%^tshow, the ability of people to intuit quality from it suffers as well.

Bad actors controlling such platforms have also learned that they can extract revenue from both controlling what you do see, as well as what they can prevent you from seeing. Like the old “protection money” Mafia schemes, they can be paid for “what doesn’t happen”, not just what does. This effectively monetizes things like “shadow-banning”, creating a market based on the suppression of information.

This is the duality that human civilization currently faces. When engaged with this overwhelming and dominantly sub-mediocre quantity of products, information, experiences, etc., everyone suffers from a proportionately overwhelming degree of “Choice Overload”. This extreme reliance on Cognitive Biases to cope with the Complexity vs Cognitive Bias Trade-off is exploited by bad actors that much more easily, as people are that much more desperate to cope with the flood. The flood itself, being so dominantly sub-mediocre, produces a mind-numbing effect, further compounding the problem.

\*Note, this is Population Dynamics in action, as over time every compounding problem is “collected” and cultivated into increasingly grotesque systems of layered exploitation. This is the reality underneath that thin narrative veneer of “Enabling” or “Empowering” people with today’s consumer AI tools, epitomized by the laughing stock that is “Vibe Coding”, which was even abandoned by the person who coined the term.
