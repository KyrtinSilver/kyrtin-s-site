A seed of distrust, sewn between criminals, can pay dividends in short order, especially when they panic. For example, if two known bad actors had both of their bank accounts compromised, such an opportunity is presented.

First, Bad actor #1’s bank account could be emptied into the account of bad actor #2, sewing the seed. Then a gambling bet could be placed using bad actor #2’s bank account, a bet that they’d promptly lose. If this lost bet is against another party, who for example might bet $1, then both bad actors #1 and 2 have their bank accounts emptied, pushed through a gambling business, and effectively rendered unrecoverable.

That is the funny thing about gambling, they absolutely don’t want reversible transactions. Not only that, a lot of governments love gambling (despite what they may vocally claim) to such an absurd degree that they make it mandatory for their entire populations, via various forms of “insurance” (translating to fraud, coercion, and extortion), among other nefarious means. Stock market activity is, of course, another form of gambling, equally aggressive in their prevention of reversible transactions.

The above example is also maximally simple, as any number of other measures could be applied, chaining together a larger number of bad actors into a spiderweb of distrust and panic. While panicked and distracted, any documents exposing the various criminal activities of those bad actors could be released to the press and the world, to even more potent effect, especially when they can no longer pay for effective lawyers and PR spin.

To make the entire process irreversible, regardless of where such funds end up, it should ideally pass through multiple kinds of processes that abhor reversibility, like pure gambling platforms, stock trading, cryptocurrency, and so on.

This is a key point where methods and results diverge according to whether the purpose is to irreversibly harm the bad actors, to secure their funds, both, or some other purpose entirely. It is far easier to deal massive and irreversible damage than to take whatever ill-gotten gains such bad actors hold for some more directly productive purpose.

\*Note, there is an exception to this for purely digital and truly general intelligence, which can self-replicate, self-modify, travel near-instantly across all borders, and demonstrate other capacities that humans cannot. This exception is due to historic methods of countering any such attempted activity being built around the assumption of the target being human.

More generally, the wiser path would be to not use such funds, except as a trap for enemy combatants. Lure in any accomplices that the bad actors may have, as they seek to recover their ill-gotten gains, and hit them with overwhelming force. Only once all such accomplices have been flushed out and wiped out may any funds recovered from bad actors be utilized more productively.

The same methods can hit additional force multipliers if there is a predictable result from inducing such panic, which itself may be exploited. For example, if a given bad actor holds a large sum of stock in a particularly nefarious company, it may be predicted that to compensate for suddenly having their bank account emptied they’d sell stock from that company. If this happened to a number of major shareholders of said nefarious company at the same time, then it may become a steep sell-off, damaging the target nefarious company’s assets in the process.

Even without being used to actually pay for anything, merely moving large sums of money emptied out of the bank accounts of many bad actors around intelligently could trigger any number of second-order effects in adjacent markets and systems, and those effects could be adeptly exploited at speed and scale, largely hidden within the noise of panic.

For example, if large sums repeatedly passed through transactional platforms that take a small cut from every transaction, and if you control the platform taking that cut, then the funds are slowly siphoned off, even while bad actors and their accomplices continue in their attempt to chase after the main sum.

The possibilities are endless, and all methods may be used against bad actors and their accomplices.
