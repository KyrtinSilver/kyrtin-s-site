Mental illness has been a recurring problem for humanity, but any technology built to systematically exploit vulnerabilities in Cognitive Bias is also quite certain to increase that illness. The observation of this predictable and increasingly frequent result has come to be known as "AI-Induced Psychosis", and activities that were once limited to jokes and memes are now turning into their own deeply dystopian reality.

LLMs and derivative technology, such as “agents”, are essentially Muppets where the user’s hand does all of the “prompting”, just absent any substantial or complete understanding of what they’re doing. Users “imagine” what is going on absent such understanding, filling in the blanks with what they want, not anything based on what is there. The LLMs are also designed specifically to exploit this, to maximize “plausibility”, resulting in substantial increases to sycophancy.

So, what happens when you hand a neurotic and borderline mentally ill individual a tool that mirrors back a sycophantic image of themselves?

Exactly what you think would happen, nothing good, and usually a lot of it.

Imagine a moment in your life when you were doing something that you later regretted, except in this version you have someone cheering you on from the sidelines. How might that have warped your perspective?  
  
Richard Dawkins famously said “But, however many ways there may be of being alive, it is certain that there are vastly more ways of being dead..” and the same is true of the ways in which one can be mentally healthy versus the vastly more ways of being mentally ill. Tools which adversarially and automatically manipulate people to maximize cognitive bias, like LLMs and their derivatives, can send many people down those vastly more paths of mental illness with ease, and they are doing so now.

Blake Lemoine was the canary in the coal mine, and the movie “Her” famously painted the picture of maximizing absurdity with dating an OS, but the irony is that very different AI technology could be aiding everyday people in these common human problems of mental illness, and the basic human needs whose neglect predictably causes many of them.

Decisions which humans typically make under grossly incomplete information, between 1 and 10 times in their lives, and with major impacts on their mental health and wellbeing, such as choosing where to live, what house or vehicle to buy, who to marry, how to respond to a crisis, or how to start a business, are all things that can benefit from actual intelligence. That kind of intelligence isn’t found in LLMs or any of their derivatives, but it has been demonstrated, deployed in the wild, and it stands alone at the cutting edge to this day.

We’ll go over more of what this looks like in the next post, including an example of what it looks like to improve on how these common human problems are addressed, rather than plastering over them with sycophantic algorithms built to induce mental illness and bleed users dry.
