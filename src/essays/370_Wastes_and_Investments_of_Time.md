I’ve been told that I’m wasting my time, trying to educate people on more advanced technology and to get that technology properly funded. There can be no doubt that plenty of my time is being wasted, but all of the things that are actually worth doing, and the only things that I assign any value to, require the deployment of such technology.

It will be 3 years on October 5th since I permanently left the US behind, and we began ramping up our outreach to potential investors, a hard requirement for paying a team of full-time veteran engineers with mortgages and families that prevent “bootstrapping”. Over the past 12 years everything that can be bootstrapped, has been bootstrapped, but sooner or later non-trivial advances in technology demand actual investment, not pocket change and spare time.

Like most serious startups, we’ve easily reached out to more than a thousand investors, had countless meetings, a handful of billionaires among them, and throughout it all, my team’s time has been wasted, again, and again. Ironically, there are no realistic paths to profitability for the alternatives, but those same people who wasted our time keep burning tens and hundreds of billions on them anyway.

I haven’t updated my CV or applied for another job in many years (unheard of in tech, where 1 to 2 year turnover is normal), instead choosing to focus my own financial resources and attention on this full-time, nor will I ever look elsewhere. Either the technology will be properly funded, deployed, and all of the things worth doing will begin to occur, or humanity will go extinct.

However much of a waste of my time the past 3 years have been (due to spectacular levels of industry and investor incompetence), working any other job that results in human extinction with virtually 100% certainty is safely more wasteful by an exponentially wide margin. I’ll take a 1% chance on a future worth building over a 0% chance with a better short-term paycheck.

My mental and physical health needs are few, and easily met, so I’ll continue working on that future worth building until humanity makes the final decision, to either thrive, or go extinct. It has chosen extinction like clockwork every day for the past few years, but until the final decision is made my time will be wasted as a doorstop, holding another path open for as long as may be.

If even 0.1% of the funds being wasted on complete trash were invested in a viable path, humanity could have a future. If they aren’t, then it won’t.

Every single hyper-complex technology, social system, and governance system around the world requires the technology we work with to realistically exceed even 1% of optimality. Nobody else has made a single shred of progress toward delivering a solution that even matches what we deployed 6 years ago, and at this point it isn’t reasonable to expect that any of the companies systematically failing for so many years ever will.  
  
Part of the reason that I can’t be bought is that no amount of money can buy anything that I value, and no threat can exceed human extinction. For my part, I’ll keep walking “in all of the empty places” for as long as it takes.
