At the intersection of Behavioral Economics and Population Dynamics, the moment that you hand an individual “immunity” to the consequences of their own actions, you run a high risk. Worse still, when you systematically hand such “immunity” to a position within Population Dynamics, you are virtually guaranteed to see those high risks come to fruition in their maximized form.

This transition from high risk to the certainty of severe consequences has played out as the chorus of many political and corresponding legal tunes, through the phrase “qualified immunity”. This could be more accurately called “maximized qualified immunity”, as the exceptions are extreme edge cases, which even then remain unlikely to result in the corresponding consequences.

Nassim Nicholas Taleb’s research and writing, including “Skin in the Game” covers the criticality of making sure that the incentive structures of any system include full accountability for one’s own actions. They must have “Skin in the Game”, otherwise you merely get Tyranny, Terrorism, and all shades of systematic exploitation. David Graeber’s works further expand on this, with historical context.

Fundamentally, no one is ever “immune” to the consequences of their actions, but people like to make up and throw around “rules” to pretend that they are. This little fantasy children’s tea party is all that shields reigning world leaders, “law enforcement officers”, and judges in the US from being prosecuted for the crimes that they commit.

Even more than 500 years ago, with the signing of the Magna Carta, it was clearly recognized that no one, not even a king, should be above the law, and yet that is precisely what systems of “qualified immunity” attempt to do. By a simple process of elimination, if those systems are unwilling to fulfill the rule of law, holding every single person accountable for their actions, then others must fulfill that role for them, taking it out of their hands. The alternative is the current lawless status quo, where immunity is bought through bribery and “loyalty” in a mafioso-style regime of organized crime.

For those who haven’t studied Population or System Dynamics, the “Peter Principle” may be a familiar historic example of the same. Under this principle, people in organizations rise to their own level of incompetence over time, because that is the predictable result of how many organizations and their incentive structures are architected.

This has even grown worse in recent years, as individuals using “GenAI” may rise to their own levels of “Superincompetence”.

The same is also true of government incentive structures, and “GenAI” does produce the same comorbidity. When individuals spreading disinformation and wielding deadly force at scale are shielded with “immunity” from the consequences of their actions, the absolute worst scum that humanity has ever created will unerringly gather under that shield.
