Every economist with a shred of credibility (such as Ed Zitron) has sounded the alarm on the AI Bubble at this point, many of them numerous times. However, the “so what do we do about it?” part has often come up short.

If left to explode under the status quo then the various bad actors who intended to create this problem in the first place will likely guide that process, extracting the maximum wealth while suffering minimal penalties for doing so.

A bubble usually pops when it runs out of money, and it runs out when enough people come to terms with reality and stop throwing money into the bottomless pit. That can come about through the violation of laws and other commitments, both philosophical and financial (Nvidia, OpenAI, Anthropic...and counting), the subsequent mass-cancellation of revenue, and Investors being swayed by both the tides of public opinion and the complete absence of any ROI.

Of course, there are other options. The bubble can pop as an ordinary economic explosive, or as a shaped charge. To extend the metaphor, some funding may be redirected toward viable technology, productively lancing this pustule on the global economy. The backlash is still going to hit certain companies, but it can be shaped to hit those bad actors much harder, sparing the rest of society some portion of the fallout that they’d otherwise have to endure.

Even a few of the bad actors realize this, as they attempt to desperately search for any way to shape that explosive backlash to hit their competitors more than themselves. Satya Nadella was especially “thirsty” in this regard, during some of his recent presentations.

Altman and Amodei have both effectively shredded all illusions of “public good” in favor of war profiteering, as they ironically attempt to escape criminal liability through a military shield, like assisting in the recent bombing of a girls’ primary school in southern Iran, where 150 were mass-murdered and another 100 maimed or injured.

“For the good of all humanity” rings hollow among such a chorus of screams, as children are mass-murdered.

Whether or not the bombing was directly the result of some \*sshole asking ChatGPT or Claude to paint their targets or pull the trigger, both companies are directly supporting the war against Iran, profiteering off of all of the mass-murder therein. That makes every single OpenAI or Anthropic employee an “enemy combatant” to every civilized country on the planet.

Murder is a crime that people understand, even though such mass-murder transitions to a statistic as people are neither able nor inclined to fully comprehend the horror of it, save a few who may never recover from facing it first-hand. Previous economic bubbles never reached this point, and horrors of equal or greater total measure may be distributed across the world when it pops, even under the best-case scenario of shaping the explosion.

I hope that our open source project may form the lance to shape that process, minimizing the damage, but only time will tell, short as that time may be.
