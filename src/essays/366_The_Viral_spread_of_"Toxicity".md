One of the most “toxic” things that social media can spread is disinformation, but a close second is absolutist ideology. In a systems design (and grounded ethics) sense, removing any fundamental option from the toolbox is virtually always the hallmark of corruption and/or incompetence, as there is virtually always a circumstance where it is the required tool for the job, even if that event only occurs once every few decades, or centuries, etc.

Even so, it is virally popular for people to ride the social waves of ideology to condemn reasonable and rational reactions on naïve ideological grounds. I don’t generally block people for this particular offense, unlike if they spread the obvious fraud of certain AI companies, but it degrades the maximum competence that I could grant them.

Some have blamed social media for a spread of “toxic” posts on the subject of an individual that virtually nobody had even heard of before his death. I wouldn’t even keep a LinkedIn if it weren’t a job requirement for me, so I can’t speak to those theoretical posts, but the counter-narrative of ideology has been highly visible since that event. I’ve said it before (with varying degrees of sarcasm) and I’ll say it again, that “I’ll never condone non-violence”.

Non-violence as an absolutist ideology is complete bunk, as even the most peaceful society in the world still has a variety of forms of violence, many institutionalized, none of those institutions “just” (justice), though sometimes it is the tool that is required. Revolution, for example, is actually required far more often than it is applied.

If anyone actually wanted to greatly reduce the frequency with which violence was the optimal tool, there are things that can be done to make that happen (ideological circle-%^&\*s on social media not being one of them). The greatest opportunity to that effect is the technology that my team works to get deployed, as it greatly raises the bar on intelligence, speed, strategy, and adaptability, changing the optimality of much of the toolbox in the process.

For example, recognizing problems earlier, applying scalable and on-demand intelligence to them, and instantly coordinating across all available assets, with the ability to handle hyper-complexity, means catching many problems at a prevention stage, not the current norm of post-destruction remediation.

In summary, if you see people posting and sharing an ideological circle-$%^& on any social platform, recognize that it is likely every bit as “toxic” as whatever they are railing against, and simply walk away.
