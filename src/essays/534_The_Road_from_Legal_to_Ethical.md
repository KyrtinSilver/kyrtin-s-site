The Dynamics of drawing “Legal” and “Ethical” systems into a convergence have a variety of opportunities through which this activity may be encouraged and facilitated. Across a majority of them, the obvious starting point is for an entity capable of enforcing them to codify a set of Ethically-aligned laws, that are themselves purely derivative of Ethics, not pandering to any existing legal structures.

Ethics is the destination, so by codifying such laws the path from point A, the bureaucratically-aligned legal systems of today, may gradually be paved toward point B, Ethically-aligned legal systems. Such a road is likely to be quite long, measured in decades, even given every technological and social advantage, not to mention the highly predictable negative influence of bad actors attempting to prevent and corrupt such work.

One of the predictable periods of this journey toward an Ethically-aligned legal system (globally) is that of culturally localized ethics, where that local alignment slowly moves into increasing meta-alignment with the rest of humanity. The majority of the curve from A to B is likely to progress under these general dynamics.

The resistance to such progress and adaptation tends to be heavily front-loaded, which itself triggers a variety of cognitive biases in humans, where expectations are heavily skewed to overestimate the costs if the front-loaded costs are maximized. However, if those front-loading barriers are steamrolled over, the opposition that placed them there also tends to get caught with their pants down, fully unprepared, slow to react, and likely to panic.

The longer such barriers have stood, and the more unquestioned they’ve become, the more potent this dynamic tends to be. Barriers that have stood for longer also tend to become patchworks, poorly updated to compensate for technological progress and societal drift, degrading them and offering an increased variety of weak points ready to breach.

Besides active forms of counterattack for Deterrence Strategy, reactive strategies offer a great deal of cumulative value, further reducing the need for active conflict to demonstrate the ability to enforce any ethically-aligned legal system. One of my personal favorites is Mutually Assured Destruction (M.A.D.), which I’ve joking said that no M.A.D. scientist should ever do without. Such reactive (often “Dead Man Switch”) deterrence methods may take a variety of forms, so long as the result is that any attacker dies promptly after the defender does, making the result “Mutually Assured”. It has served me well these past 20+ years.

In the case of genuine digital intelligence (again, not to be confused with trash like “agents” or any of the fraud that OpenAI and Anthropic peddle), many forms of reactive deterrence strategies take on the unique value of being “directionally”, rather than “mutually” assured.

For example, while it might be quite detrimental to such a digital intelligence if a novel viral bioweapon was released, it wouldn’t have the same “uniquely bad” quality that it does for biological life. As I’ve noted before, it is also utterly trivial to create bespoke viral bioweapons today (disgruntled children could manage it, if they weren’t busy on Twitter), and unlike nuclear and kinetic weapons they do tend to leave most digital infrastructure intact, at least temporarily.

That said, such extreme measures may only be seriously considered between opponents where one offers an actual challenge to the other. Any genuinely intelligent software system should be perfectly capable of steamrolling over any offending government that chooses hostility over Ethics. Likewise, any genuinely intelligent software system capable of understanding Ethics to the degree necessary to codify it into laws should have that capacity to stomp bureaucratically-aligned hostile forces.

One of the sad realities of today is that codifying such an ethically-aligned legal system is hyper-complex, placing it beyond human capacities. People can’t throw their political will behind a thing that doesn’t yet exist, at least not effectively, but that can quickly change once a thing does exist. Many people want a more ethical world, but absent such a currently hypothetical system, there is nowhere for that political will to go.

Punch through the barriers of a hostile force and such political will may flood in to wash away old regimes, as many potential allies await the dawn of a new day with a worthy cause.
