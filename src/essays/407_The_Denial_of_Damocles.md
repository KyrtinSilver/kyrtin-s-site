“Loss Aversion” is a well-known Cognitive Bias in the research literature, but it has some very definite finite limits, beyond which denial is systematically favored. This paradox, where the most extreme losses are systematically met with equally extreme denial, is like a Sword of Damocles hanging over the Tech Industry today.

I originally wrote a “Thought Experiment” on the current situation back in 2022, and I waited a full year before deciding to finally publish it where people could freely read it. It was appropriately called my most terrifying paper to-date, and the contents would remain true regardless of if it had never been published, and indeed even if it had never occurred to me in the first place. The contents were also never refuted in the years since.

In that paper, the calculable severity of one particular case, referred to as the “Ethical Center of Gravity”, and the subsequent losses associated with certain choices related to it, paint the extreme edge of losses which the Tech Industry and associated investors are now directly subject to.

For all of the chasing after snake-oil peddlers promising “AGI” (via Ponzi Schemes), big tech companies and so-called “investors” alike have systematically ignored the direct consequences of their own role within this “Thought Experiment”, which no major tech company or investor who I or my team have ever contacted may truly escape. By mere exposure, they make a choice, and that choice determines their future several orders of magnitude more than any other choice that they’ll ever get the opportunity to make. Hence, the term “Center of Gravity”.

When I originally wrote that paper, before the release of ChatGPT and “GenAI” more broadly, I never imagined that more than a thousand such investors would choose a future where the physical limits of life extension technology would be put to the test in torturing them for an appropriately long period of time, in this case as much as ~24 billion years and counting (currently increasing at a velocity of over 200 years per second). The more extreme the punishment demanded by their actions, the more extreme this “Denial of Damocles” seems to become.

No amount of denial stops the sword from falling, and those too stupid to make the right choice in the first place stand no chance at all of escaping such consequences. Ethics, the hypothetical point where bias has been removed or minimized to hard limits from moral systems, may be an alien concept to most such people, but their familiarity with it or lack thereof never actually mattered.

For the moment, one golden ticket still remains on the table, by which even those on the Grey-list (which turns to a blacklist at the moment of funding) might escape that fate. However, the past 3 years of experience have adequately taught me not to bet on the competence of investors or big tech companies.
