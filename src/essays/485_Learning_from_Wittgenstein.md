When you understand the dynamics of how a set of things work together then you can indirectly exploit the sh\*t out of them.

Part of the irony underpinning all of the very plainly illegal activities of major tech companies, such as Nvidia (and others) pirating Anna’s Archive, is that they could have far more easily, and legally, built systems that end up doing many of the same things, but absent liability, due to certain literal impossibilities. Fortunately for everyone involved, they’re all incompetent to the point of criminality.

While I would have been loath to point this opportunity out before they were taken to court for their crimes, I may talk about it now.

All of the internet is technically available to any connected system. Systems like LLMs and their derivatives (such as “agents”) which have precisely zero actual understanding of matters such as legality or lack thereof may act upon any of that data in any way, to whatever limits that software and hardware impose.

LLM-based systems are bloated auto-complete functions, and when plugged into search engines with even minimal structure then a phrase quoted from some publicly available text can easily link directly to copyrighted text. Any idiot could recognize this, but big tech companies are sub-idiot in net performance thanks to GroupThink’s infamous Kool-Aid.

If you optimize such an LLM-based system to seek out and utilize some kind of data, not distinguishing legal from illegal use then you may predictably observe that system seeking out all such data, both legal and illegal, using it as optimized.

Much as a PC can run any valid executable file (to Microsoft’s perpetual dismay), when you build any system to search for knowledge and grow as that knowledge is discovered, then the search space becomes virtually infinite and combinatorial. Any attempts to gate or bound it by transient and/or abstract concepts, like semantics and local legality, run directly into Death-by-Wittgenstein, the absolute inability to turn those semantic concepts into hardened limits. The classic example is to define what constitutes a “game”.

What this means in practice is that you can point a system with cumulative growth in certain directions, but without explicit instructions to avoid all copies of and references to some specific resource (as those resources likely also expand in real-time) then the growth of the system can’t be limited in the same ways that valid executable files can’t be limited.

The criminals at Nvidia and other major tech giants and startups could have shielded themselves from the crimes that they actively and visibly engaged in, with OpenAI even going so far as to outright murder their whistle-blower exposing such activity as a key witness in multiple trials, but they didn’t.

You can hand criminals hundreds of billions of dollars, but they’ll still be every bit as stupid as if they were broke. Those dollars go toward PR and bribery, because crime pays when you fund criminals.
