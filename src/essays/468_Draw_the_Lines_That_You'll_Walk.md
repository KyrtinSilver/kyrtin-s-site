In order to “Walk the Line”, you must first draw that line. The reason for this is rooted in the cognitive bias research literature, in that unwritten guidelines tend to both bend and drift, with no accountability, record, or other hard metrics by which they may be judged.

This becomes at least an order of magnitude more critical whenever there is another party present who stands to benefit from your failure, drift, bending, etc., which is frequently if not usually the case. Examples of this form of malevolent activity abound in the AI domain, such as the popular delusions of “exponential progress”, propogated virally even when all credible metrics point to virtual stagnation of the same.

Besides the threat of external parties attempting to fool you, nobody is better at fooling you than yourself, particularly when you emotionally “want” something to be true. Our Cognitive Biases simplify our perceptions of the world and decisions, guided by the human motivational system, emotions.

Drawing many firm lines adds structure by which both external and internal influences may be caught, quantified, and mitigated. Even as your mind is constantly tempted to believe the things that you want to be true, countless marketing departments across the world hold the explicit goal of fooling you into whatever beliefs serve their purposes.

For example, take two scenarios, with and without the structure of drawn lines. In both scenarios the marketing of AI makes claims by pointing to some new “benchmark” or “research” (from companies that do no actual research, like Anthropic):

Scenario 1: Lines Drawn: The new claims are measured against hard pre-determined criteria, with the credibility and methodology of material challenging those lines scrutinized. This is “data-driven”.

Scenario 2: No Lines Drawn: The new claims and their narrative act as the primary structure, bending according to the prior beliefs and desires of the perceiving party. This greatly reduces the upper level of scrutiny which may be applied to them, if indeed any is at all. This is “narrative-driven”.

Many lines must be drawn for a solid defence, since as Sun Tzu pointed out long ago, an enemy (external or internal) will attack from whatever side is least defended, and as Population Dynamics dictates, that enemy will be the most likely to survive, thrive, and attack again.

Two common narrative-driven attack vectors in the AI domain these past 3 years have been fake benchmarks and “research”, such as the “FrontierMath”, “ARC-AGI”, and “Sparks of AGI” fraud from OpenAI, or pretty much anything that Anthropic has ever done. METR and SWE-bench Verified are also popular debunked benchmarks.

However, credible experts have no trouble recognizing these attacks for what they are, as the lines that they draw control the structure by which claims are judged, and such attacks require narratives to act as the backbone by which they are judged.
