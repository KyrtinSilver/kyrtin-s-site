Some of the most egregious cases of Cognitive Bias in action come from zealous individuals naively attempting to wield them as a cudgel, or turn them into “check-box qualifications”. In the case of such qualifications, arbitrary requirements are used to gate any given set of benefits or group membership, designating an exclusion zone, even when the criteria for inclusion make no actual sense in context. This leads to the practice of “Tokenism”, to the direct detriment of both each group seeking inclusion and the intended purpose of such measures.

One of the common ways that such check-box qualifications fail is that they are context-blind. For example, my team has operated pro-bono for years, with participants self-selecting, choosing to continue the work and research, or not. That self-selection dynamic is completely different from hiring, where the individual has control over the choice to participate, or not. Check-boxes are blind to this.

Another common and unforgivable oversight is known as “Insensitivity to Sample Size”, meaning that if less than 30 members of a company, such as a Founding or Executive team, is being evaluated, the results won’t meet the baseline statistical criteria for being distinct from random noise, aka “statistical significance” (not to be confused with “actual significance”, which is a much higher bar than “not random”).

Actual diversity also takes into account far more forms of diversity than may be seen from a distance of 50 meters. Check-box qualification systems enforce an arbitrary and extremely narrow subset, in a context-blind manner, causing direct detriment to all other forms of diversity, toward the alleged benefit of the one target, but even that falls flat in practice.

Rigid and context-blind systems are by their very nature built explicitly to oppose diversity, they serve as funnels with some conformity-oriented goal, no matter how naively well-meaning that goal may be. By taking this approach, they come to embody the very problems that they were intended to combat.

My team is self-selected, diverse across 4 continents worth of culture, neurodiverse, and diverse in philosophy, skills, and experience. We have many forms of diversity, including the one that matters most, Diversity of Thought, and the ability to work together as a team through Collective Intelligence.

Actual diversity also becomes orders of magnitude easier to architect and deliver post-funding, and given a sufficiently flexible framework for calculating diversity more meaningful measures could be applied as companies pass funding and growth milestones.

The political anti-diversity criminal regimes will flail for a time, then burn to a crisp, but those seeking actual diversity also have some growing up to do. Cognitive Biases operate effortlessly given any arbitrary aim, and it is only through explicitly architecting systems to mitigate and bypass them that they may be overcome.
