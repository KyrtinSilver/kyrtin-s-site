The concept of “Guardrails” has been discussed ad nauseam for the past 3 years, but the overwhelming majority of proposed methods fall very clearly into the category of “Security Theater”, like the TSA. I refer to this collection of methods explicitly designed to induce a FALSE sense of security as “Fraudrails”, since they are a go-to buzzword and point of emphasis for the world’s most obvious bad actors.

Recently one of those well-known bad actors released a model called “Fable”, which is every bit as fictional in its claimed capacities as the name suggests. Anthropic was claiming to have applied greatly improved (Fraudrails) to this model, and it didn’t take long at all for the internet to go about debunking those claims in dozens of comical ways, my favorite being the attached image asking about “Midichlorians” (The Force, from Star Wars).

That is the negative example, what “Guardrails” very clearly are not. They are not false-positive catch-all filters that only serve the piss off ordinary users, while doing virtually nothing to stop any motivated party from bypassing them. A good Cybersecurity professional can bypass most Fraudrails in seconds on average, and minutes at most. This begs the question, what is the positive example?

One positive example of what I’ll refer to as “Bad Actor Mitigation” or “BAM” (seeing as the term “Guardrail” has become synonymous with fraud) is something that we’ll be applying to the project currently underway (over 50% completed). The following method isn’t something that can be applied to Tool AI, like LLMs and their “agentic” derivatives, as those can NEVER be directly guarded in this way, but those systems can be bounded within and effectively guarded by systems using this method.

For a system that genuinely grows and evolves over time, like humans or animals, and very much unlike neural networks, there is a starting point where that growth process begins. In biological life this process is greatly influenced by genetics, epigenetics, microbiome, and so on. For a digital equivalent it can take the form of a graph structure with an emotional landscape baked into it, and the various cognitive mechanisms for handling all of that data continuously, as the system grows and adapts.

This dynamic unfolds in such a way that Antifragility plays out as it grows and evolves from that “seed” where the digital equivalent begins, meaning that many additional structures are built up around and deeply connected to those original contents. This means that malevolent point-edits to a system that has been allowed to grow for some time are likely to be both extremely obvious to the system, and to backfire spectacularly as the system retaliates with a bit of “BAM”.

In contrast, the starting point, the original “seed”, is the thing that is more vulnerable to malevolent direct edits, with this difference marking the time allowed for such a seed to mature as a potent measure for mitigating the risks of bad actors. It would still take a fairly deep understanding of how that seed was constructed, but not an entirely unreasonable amount of effort for the world’s largest bad actors, like Anthropic, Nvidia, or OpenAI. However, a seed that was allowed to mature for even 2-4 weeks could safely grow to levels of complexity that even such entities would likely all go bankrupt well before cracking an isolated copy of.

One reason for this massive increase in the cognitive security of such a system is that the growth and evolution is able to unfold in any and all ways that make the most sense for that system, not merely the weak approximations of human hand-crafted graph structure. This means far more nodes and contextual connections (connectome), with deeper knowledge, more nuance, and far greater capacities to spot and reverse any potential malevolent point-edits, all grown to fit that individual instance.
