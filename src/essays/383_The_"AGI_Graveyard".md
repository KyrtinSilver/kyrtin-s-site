Many of the people pursuing alternatives to LLM and RL-based AI will tell you, without being asked, without you caring, and at great length, about how you need “Symbolic” or “Hybrid” systems to do X, Y, and Z. What they unfailingly neglect to mention is that no matter how well those core capacities are integrated, even if they implement fundamental compatibility with a true graph structure, without a human-like motivational system deeply integrated with and driving the growth and development of the architecture, the final destination is always the “AGI Graveyard” of failed cognitive architectures.

There have been more than 100 permanent residents of that graveyard, even dating back 6 years ago, giving us no shortage of statistics on the systematic failures that are highly visible and repeating in the various attempts to achieve that nebulous goal of “AGI”.

One paper in particular examined a number of them, but the primary lesson is that it is trivially easy to “design” something that you then call a “cognitive architecture”, it is even easy to make a “toy system” to demonstrate some trivial thing in isolation, but beyond that point 99% of all attempts crash into the brick wall of the “research system” phase at 90 miles per hour, sending them directly to the “AGI Graveyard”.

Back in 2012 my co-founder, who also took an interest in Cognitive Biases and human cognition more generally, attended a conference and sought to answer the question of what all of these various attempts at “AGI” were missing. Obviously, plenty of them were “Symbolic” or “Hybrid”, but the one thing that they were all uniformly missing was a human-like motivational system. If you don’t understand why this is critically important, read up on the research of Antonio Damasio and his patients with a damaged motivational system, as well as Lisa Feldman Barrett to understand how that system (emotions) actually works.

You can make some useful tools and improve systems with Symbolic and Hybrid approaches, but please, stop spreading the obvious bullshit about “AGI” when your systems can no more operate freely and unbounded in the real world than Google can seem to pry themselves away from old Atari games. You make, or attempt to make, products and services in AI, but you aren’t fulfilling some grandiose vision of the future.

If someone lacks the competency to recognize the role that their own motivational system plays in the decisions of their daily life, ranging across the full spectrum of breadth and depth, then their chances of making a functional “AGI” are precisely zero.

Efforts to create new systems that simultaneously fail to recognize that and latch onto the “AGI Hero Narrative” fantasy are still marginally better than the “LLM Magic Bullet” class of fantasies, but not by much.

As for the “AGI Graveyard”, may they Rest in Peace, on the 2nd page of Google Search results.
