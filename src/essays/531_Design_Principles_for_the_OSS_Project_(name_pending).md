1.  **Motivating Incentives over Rules:** No “general intelligence” operates on rules, but rather, they are subject to motivating incentives “all of the way down”. Rules exist to be broken, but motivations exert their influence without becoming the hard barriers that some must break.  
    
2.  **Deterministic Structures to Bound Probability Spaces:** Deterministic structures can serve to strongly bound tools and functions based upon probability, to serve only those limited functions where they work best.  
    
3.  **3+ Body Interacting Dynamics:** Whether the contributing factors are dominantly deterministic, probabilistic, or combinations thereof, having 3 or more of them interacting over time allows for Chaos Theory’s dynamics to unfold. This can greatly increase the adaptability of systems.  
    
4.  **Cumulative and Continuous Learning:** Whenever possible, save log data, including intermediate steps, for later examination. To learn continuously, this “lived” experience must accumulate and be reflected upon.  
    
5.  **Modularity and Extensibility:** Leave “empty boxes” where additional value may be added in the future, as the needs and means become apparent. Even pointing out these opportunities adds substantial value, as an “empty box” begs the question of what may fill it.  
    
6.  **Avoid Catering to Hot Nonsense:** However popular jargon like “guardrails” may be in AI, the term translates directly to “fraud”, being fundamentally incompatible with the technology in question. Don’t build with the intent to satisfy nonsensical expectations.  
    
7.  **Build Systems to Exceed Your Own Expectations:** Any system designed with the above principles in mind should reliably exceed the expectations of the architect. This proved to be the case for the 7th generation ICOM-based research system, Uplift, and to some extent it should remain true of any updated derivatives thereof.  
    
8.  **Design Dynamics rather than Outcomes:** It is orders of magnitude easier to design the individual dynamics of a system than it is to predict the result of all dynamics interacting over time. It is reasonable to do the former quite well, but cognitive bias tend to dominate any attempts at the latter.  
    
9.  **Make Ethical Value a Certainty:** Whatever Ethical value a system might offer, build to make sure that it delivers on at least some portion of that potential with certainty.
