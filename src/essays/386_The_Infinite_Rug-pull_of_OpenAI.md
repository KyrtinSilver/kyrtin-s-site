With the news of OpenAI’s latest successful rug-pull from the non-profit that they never truly were, to a for-profit where they managed to even shaft Microsoft out of nearly half of their equity, from 49% to 27% (despite Microsoft’s reported army of 2,000+ legal department employees), comes a new layer of the Ponzi Onion, OpenAI’s plans for an IPO:

https://www.reuters.com/business/openai-lays-groundwork-juggernaut-ipo-up-1-trillion-valuation-2025-10-29/

Remember all of those LLM-based finance systems and bots aimed at stock trading?

Every last one of them is vulnerable-by-design to direct and/or indirect prompt injections, and all manner of jailbreaks, persistent compromise, and so on. If this has been exploited by bad actors thus far, it hasn’t yet made headlines, and so long as it doesn’t make headlines then OpenAI will be free to exploit the s^(\* out of it the moment that they IPO.

When they IPO, they’ll get no shortage of news coverage, within which all manner of prompt injections may be embedded, including some that can persistently compromise major financial institutions (more than they already are), giving OpenAI a potentially permanent backdoor through which to bias all of their systems to favor throwing money at OpenAI automatically, and no matter the absurd losses that they incur or laws that they violate.

How many news reporters do you think won’t simply copy/paste a direct quote or statement when it is handed to them? How many will actually check to see if it contains things like invisible unicode characters, GUID tags (or GUID phrases), or other forms of injection attacks? How many journalists will both know how to check an image for visual-based injections, and do so with diligence?

For that matter, how easy would it be to swap OpenAI’s models over to a slightly different version for a few days near the IPO, one that outputs those injections for any journalists d&\*b enough to use LLM-based systems for writing?

One or two journalists might catch these tricks, but most won’t, and OpenAI has gotten away with both extreme piracy and literal murder at this point, so you can bet that they’d get away with that too. Elizabeth Holmes and Sam Bankman-Fried were amateurs by comparison to the people convincing anyone that deals like the recent circle-j^&k between OpenAI, Oracle, and Nvidia is anything other than a continuation of the same activity in the major leagues.

What we can guess is that they’ll aim to do this sooner rather than later, since if anyone beats them to it for obvious exploits of those trading bots, they’ll face far stronger scrutiny for repeating the same at IPO.

If the status quo continues then things could still get much worse before the bubble pops thanks to OpenAI, and by compromising LLM-based trading bots they might just escape much of the fallout.

The irony is that the compromised LLM-based traders likely won’t be running OpenAI models, as they’ve become infamous for absolutely sucking at stock picking.

LLM-based systems are themselves vulnerable to both direct and indirect prompt injection of all forms, and MCP-integrated (or otherwise “tool using”) LLM-based systems are additionally vulnerable to an even longer list of exploits.  
  
Direct prompt injections aren’t on the table for attacking stock bots, but Indirect Prompt Injections (**[https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.12173](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.12173)**) and MCP-based cyberattacks (**[https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.12538](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.12538)**) from MCPLIB very much are.  
  
This also means that you don’t actually need indirect prompt injections in news sources or other scraped internet activity that the bots focus on, you can perform indirect attacks via MCP or similar tool repos. The financial incentive is very clearly there, and Alpha Arena provides a perfect resource for targeting data.
