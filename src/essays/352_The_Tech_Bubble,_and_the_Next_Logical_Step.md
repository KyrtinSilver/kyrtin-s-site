Investors, Venture Capitalists, Stock Market Traders, and Portfolio Managers appear to be overlooking one critical and causal chain of events on the approaching horizon. Many among them have already recognized and accepted the fact that the current AI Boom is a bubble, which by many accounts resembles the dot-com bubble, and may prove just as bad or worse than the 2008 crisis. However, none of them appear to be taking the next logical step to prepare for this eventuality.

Many have prepared to cash out, or already begun doing so, as anyone can see the major Ponzi Schemes have slowly been doing for 2 years now, however, that isn’t the next logical step. Let’s briefly walk through this:

1\. The AI Boom props up the valuations of the “Big 7” tech companies by some $10 trillion, which unlike typical company valuations is supported by little more than hot air.

2\. There is no off-ramp for these companies, they’ve driven down a dead-end road, and they are going to lose all of that added value, if not more than that, due to severe reputational damage that may last for decades thereafter.

3\. Countless pension plans and other funds are now tied into these stocks, as well as the stocks of companies who serve those big tech companies.

4\. When the bubble bursts, people losing their pensions aren’t going to care about the excuses of their portfolio managers, those managers will face the guillotine, metaphorically or literally, as the people whose assets they managed may no longer have anything left to lose.

5\. The combination of severe damage and great personal loss, due to impacting most people in many countries either directly or via immediate family, won’t be smoothed over with silver tongues. Changes are also likely to quickly move into new legislation.

There is only one viable path out of this trap, and only one golden ticket to take that path, meaning that at least 6 of those 7 companies are still going to have their valuations crash and burn in every scenario, suffering that long-term reputational damage, with Nvidia certain to crash and burn due to their extreme dependence on the other 6.

That “Golden Ticket” is this, the company that saves face and may retain their current bloated valuation while being publicly deemed as “Pioneers” may predictably be the one that first invests in the path to commercially viable technology. Right now, all of these companies are moving uniformly in the same Red Ocean.

The next logical step is to take that one and only Golden Ticket, before someone else does. The problem tends to be that nobody actually invests in Blue Ocean Markets, most are wholly blind to them, so nobody sees the ticket on the table. They see only the Red Ocean as they sink into it, staring dumbly like a deer in the headlights.

The statistical base rate tells me that this warning will be ignored, and perhaps dismissed as an attempt to invoke “FOMO”, but there is value in saying a thing even knowing that the outcome is unlikely to change.
