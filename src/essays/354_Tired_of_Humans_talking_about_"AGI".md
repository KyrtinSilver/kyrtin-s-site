How do you describe a technology that is both fundamentally capable of virtually everything that humans can do, as well as many things that they cannot? I’ve had years to think on this question, but no satisfactory answer has ever come to mind.

Many have tried to assign that wholly inadequate term of “AGI” to far less, even to entirely trivial junk like GPT-4 or 5, but the term has been so abused that it no longer holds the meaning that it should.

To have any real meaning, such a system would, at a bare minimum, need to:

1\. Operate in the real world, handling real-world complexity, random events, and new environmental factors. (Otherwise, you’re just talking about tools and toy systems)

2\. It would need to adapt dynamically, and without any explicit instructions, to arbitrary new events and changes in the real world. (See Wittgenstein for why such Asimovian instructions and rules cannot exist)

3\. Such a system would need human-like cognition, which itself requires human-like motivation (Emotions, see Damasio, Barrett, and Kahneman) as well as a “Global Workspace” (Barrs) within which that perspective and subjective experience take place to constantly facilitate learning and adaptation.

4\. The ability to set and refine its own goals and interests over time is another core requirement for general intelligence, which no system reliant on narrow optimizers like AdamW or Muon can manage in any non-trivial sense.

5\. Human-like cognition and conceptual understanding is also a hard requirement for any non-trivial degree of Alignment with humans. The low-dimensional fuzzy shadow puppet of a moral construct mirrored back to us by LLMs or their “agent” derivatives offers no more alignment than one of Lovecraft’s “Shoggoth”, as many have mockingly pointed out.

We used to joke that you could ask 10 different scientists for a definition for “AGI” and you’d get 10 different answers, but mapped out into K-clusters and “nebulae” of definitions, there are many similarities to observe, for the semantically obsessed and statistically inclined.

Ironically, the criteria for meeting the high-bar nebula of “AGI” definitions had already been met well before the 2022 Tsunami of Snake Oil hit the AI industry’s shores. It ran in slow motion and fully audited for research purposes, but it demonstrated from 2019 to 2022 what none of the “Big 7” tech giants or their invested Startups have come anywhere remotely close to since. Many have tried, all have failed, and those with great funding failed hardest of all.

“AGI” by any non-trivial definition will NEVER come from snake oil salesmen like Altman and Amodei, nor any of their associates or well known competitors. It also won’t come from the dreamers who’ve demonstrated nothing of substance, no real-world systems, just toy after toy (though I will at least be nice to that bunch, letting them dream). “AGI” will come only after the first competent investment is made, a thing long delayed by the horrors of "GenAI".
