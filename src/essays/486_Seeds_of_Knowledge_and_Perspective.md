One of the more interesting research frontiers is the study of how dynamically grown and evolved cumulative systems develop differently according to their contents, synergies, ordering, the motivational values attached to them, as well as how they’re architected.

For example, in the ICOM-based seed material I’m building for the Open Source project now, I’m combining knowledge of Stoicism, Buddhism, Atheism, Democracy, Socialism, Communism, Libertarianism, and even Capitalism. Many synergize, and few are ever “purely” one thing or another.

Since the dynamics are combinatorial across a graph structure, then there is still a lot of guesswork/intuition factoring into exactly how knowledge of these concepts is likely to combine and synergize, given any specific set of contents, motivational values, and the ordering of all parts. Intuitions can be improved through practice, experimentation, and subsequent research, but it remains firmly within the domain of hyper-complexity, levels of complexity exceeding human cognitive bandwidth.

Stoicism, Buddhism, and Atheism all share a fair number of practical features and methods within such structure, with most (if not the most vocal examples) who follow each philosophy respectively holding dominantly compatible views.

Democracy, Socialism, and Communism are an example closer to the opposite, hypothetical concepts with less compatible views. Libertarianism and Capitalism hold some crossover with those hypothetical concepts, but no shortage of ways to merge them to some degree have already appeared absent the advantages of solving for hypercomplexity.

As the dynamics for ICOM-based technology focus on cumulative (and refined) value and collective intelligence across such a growing structure, more polished seed data with more kinds of value added to it (such as motivational data and metadata) further improve the seed of knowledge that functions as a starting point for growth. Unlike probabilistic next-token systems, this process isn’t greedy, so more data is no substitute for high quality data with the connectome value of metadata and motivational values.

I’ve begun reaching out to sources who’ve written high quality material with citations covering some of these topics, to get their permission to use their articles for the Open Source project, and I can already offer my thanks to several for approving this request. The words of those who accept this request will form the seed of knowledge from which the systems of this project may grow.

Once the project’s first phase is completed, they’ll also be free to modify the seeds, experimenting with the various ways in which that growth may be modified, including running a local instance with more of their own writing baked into the seed to drive further alignment toward their own philosophies and culture.

We are still in need of one more person to build the “graded loop” segment for the project, but the rest of the work has begun. Tags are welcome.
