One of the most difficult lessons for people to learn is that sometimes a self-selection process must take place BEFORE any default may be applied in a sequence.

For example, since Dune memes have been making the rounds again (with people tagging me), we’ll take the test of the Gom Jabbar. In this test, someone must demonstrate fundamental higher cognitive capacities before they earn the default consideration of being “human”.

I apply the same method, constantly, in many ways which people have read time and time again. The basic idea is that you offer one or more cliffs, such as knee-jerk animal reactions, for people to throw themselves off of at an early stage. This greatly reduces the pointless burdens that may be placed upon your time.

The recent method applied in my latest paper on Human Rights and Freedoms also takes this a step further, establishing a “universally unpalatable” baseline by steamrolling over both “sacred” and “taboo” concepts by design, and with indifference. This is unusual, in that it isn’t designed to “persuade” anyone, as persuasion is usually a synonym for manipulation in practice. In other words, it avoids specific “persuasive” Cognitive Biases.

Rather, you can think of this approach like audio or image quality across format conversions. You need to start with a high quality file and convert directly from that high quality baseline each time. The highest quality version may see little direct use, but it is required for all derivatives to avoid severe degradation.

A good research paper with certain forms of cognitive bias greatly reduced or removed operates in the same way, and if anyone has tried the same before I expect that most of the peer reviewers probably had a stroke, not just “Reviewer **#2**“. Consequently, this means Survivorship Bias favors no such papers being published.

Of course, I apply this sequential method with self-selection to life in general, as a strong “pruning” force to optimize my time toward more productive investments.

I offer all who read my posts the Gom Jabbar, and those who throw themselves upon the needle never waste my time again. Let humans be human, invest your time in them, and point the rest to the nearest cliff, at the earliest stage. Empathy is a worthy thing to cultivate and offer, but generally only following self-selection.

In methodological terms, always try to remember the critical importance of properly ordering your sequences. In any sequential process, this tends to make a difference measured in orders of magnitude.
