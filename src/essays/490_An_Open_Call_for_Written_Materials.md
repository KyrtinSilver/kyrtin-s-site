I’m putting out an open call for writing (preferably in an academic format, meaning that it includes citations) for concepts and subjects that could be used without copyright limitations for the project described here: [https://github.com/KyrtinSilver/Open-Source-Project-Phase-1-Summary/tree/main](https://github.com/KyrtinSilver/Open-Source-Project-Phase-1-Summary/tree/main)

Beyond Wikipedia, very few resources on the internet are actually searchable with such unrestricted licenses for use, and so even widely known and discussed concepts lack the most fundamental texts going over them under these conditions.

For example, I’m still working on securing permissions to use texts going over topics such as:

1\. Democracy

2\. Socialism

3\. Communism

4\. Capitalism

5\. Atheism

6\. Buddhism

The purpose of these texts is to lay out fundamental knowledge of these concepts that may be integrated into a graph structure, and the basic knowledge of a different kind of AI system. This entire process is also explicitly designed to respect copyrights, not simply scraping the internet absent the consent of authors.

Moreover, the written works of the authors who give their permission become the high-confidence material from which a system’s growth begins. Like the words of a parent, this makes what they have to say of far greater impact than others, at least for the initial portion of the growth curve.

There is also a need for many high-quality texts going over any number of other subjects, with the permission of authors and copyright limitations being the limiting factors. These apply to numerous domains, as well as the concepts crossing over them, with a particular emphasis on Cybersecurity and Medicine domains for the initial two seeds to be built.

Once again, non-technical contributors are also welcome, by engaging in an associative exercise of reading any of said material and then listing out a number of terms and concepts tied to it. This exercise populates “metadata” related to the written material, which can be converted into the connectome of a graph structure. This is also a form of collective intelligence, taken from a structural angle.

Likewise, the academic format of including citations is an extension of this, adding to the graph structure with links further supporting and expanding documents and bodies of related research.

So please, tag your favorite researchers, professors, or other authors with such written works who may wish to contribute to the project. Personally, I’d love to build a system with Nassim Nicholas Taleb’s attitude.

\*Even some works of fiction may be considered (by authors of some note), as that could allow the systems to develop some very entertaining use of references and related quirks.
