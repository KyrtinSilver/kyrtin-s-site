Real transparency is messy and random, which most people still intuitively realize. However, what people think of as “transparency” today generally falls into 3+ categories, the real thing only being one among them.

You have the corporately curated “C-transparency”, where most lies come in the form of omission and selection, the corporate lawyer’s domain.

There is also the PR department’s “P-transparency”, where everything is polished to follow some narrative, even when that process completely unhinges it from reality.

Lastly, we come to “R-transparency”, where you get real, random, and subsequently messy transparency into reality, because that is how reality is.

People understand that R-transparency is the only truth among them, but humans are so cognitive biased in favor of narratives, confirmation bias, and array of related biases that they’ll hold this understanding in cognitive dissonance, still accepting the narrative, as it is far easier to remember and work with than messy reality.

You get P-transparency from the representatives and senior staff of major tech companies, varying degrees of lies to boost stock prices, startup valuations for raising funds, or the sales of defective products and services. This is also the only form of transparency that is optimized to search engines and news feed algorithms, making most people over 100x more likely to see it than anything else.

You get C-transparency in the “Terms of Service (ToS)” that you probably don’t read more than 1-10% of, if that. This is also by design, as the meaning is often obfuscated behind dense legalize language to mask their horrifying intentions.

R-transparency is a bit Autistic, just unfiltered, random, difficult to fit into simple narratives. This is also actually why PR departments exist, because such reality often has to be severely distorted to fit into concise and coherent narratives.

Much of society has also begun to realize in 2025 just how deep into “unreality” humanity has moved, where the lies and BS wash out everything else in a tsunami of volume. This is largely thanks to the algorithms that prioritize them, as well as money backing them. Residents of the digital world now live in a garbage dump, surrounded by trash at all times, unable to escape the smell of it. Is it any wonder why an increasing margin now seek to put it all to an end?

One of the remaining litmus tests for honest dealings today is that R-transparency looks nothing like the other two, as they are each distinctly optimized to a purpose, and R-transparency is, by definition, not optimized at all.

If someone (or a platform) is feeding you one of the optimized forms of “transparency”, they are your enemy.
