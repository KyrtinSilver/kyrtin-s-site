Imagine filing a $100,000 application and having it be rejected, with no real transparency as to why.

While I loath even checking typical news, today brings something that clearly points to which way the wind is blowing for AI/ML in the geopolitical sense, something so blatantly absurd that I and my team thought it was fake at first. A quick search of news outlets showed that it was not: [https://apnews.com/live/donald-trump-news-updates-9-19-2025](https://apnews.com/live/donald-trump-news-updates-9-19-2025)

The US talks a big game about AI/ML and leading in technology, but Silicon Valley has peddled virtually nothing but trash for years, and barring the gates against external talent with a $100,000 annually recurring gamble on every single non-US citizen or permanent resident employee is a sufficiently final nail in that coffin. Knowing the source of this absurdity, there are no doubt 100 more similar nails just waiting to be hammered in, but they are redundant for the purpose of this burial of the US tech industry.

I’m reminded of the words of Emo Philips, paraphrased: “It shows that you have self-destructive tendencies.”, “Well what do you recommend?”, “Go for it!”

Given his historic tactics, this is likely just a prompt for the industry to throw more bribe-money his way, as well as ceding more “power” (the idiot’s version of it) to his cronies. This was seen with tariffs, and will likely be seen many times again moving forward, as his strategy toolbox is quite shallow.

That said, such bribe money buys no allies in his regime, and versed as the tech industry may be with lobbyist systems of bribery and patronage, they’re completely out of their depth now, it doesn’t work on a true dictator. There is a certain degree of Schadenfreude to watching one corrupt and deeply unethical party get pummeled by another, but the aftermath offers no solace to anyone, the light at the end of that tunnel is an oncoming train.

The US has objectively been one of the worst places to live in the world among developed or “developing” countries for at least a couple of decades now, with so-called “developing” countries steadily passing them by during that time. Cost of living and quality of life more generally have historically mattered little to investors for where they put their funds, but extreme, absurd, and unpredictable moves like this hang a guillotine over any investments in the US.

My team has already been considering reforming our company (moving all equity and SAFEs over to the new company) in another country for the benefits that certain other countries offer, but the increasing detriments and unpredictability of those detriments in the US offer at least equally compelling reasons to make the move.

To those still presently trapped in the US, I’ll quote the famous words of Charlie Chaplin (1940, “The Great Dictator” speech): “And so long as men die, liberty will never perish.”
