“Economic Warfare” versus “Traditional Warfare” is a distinction without a difference. Both are warfare, where the means and goals may vary, but the end result is often equally damaging, just in very different ways. The damage from Economic Warfare is far more insidious, and often persistent. For example:

-   Economic Warfare adds a vast array of false narratives, cognitive traps, by which modern slaves are procured. Some of these are also viral, allowing their damage to spread in ways that the bullets and bombs of traditional warfare do not.  
      
    
-   While Traditional Warfare is obvious, Economic Warfare can wear many masks, holding “plausible deniability” even when caught, and often operating for long periods relatively unnoticed by general populations.  
      
    
-   This allows Economic Warfare to recruit many accomplices, both knowing and unknowing, as although most humans today are cognitively biased against participating in traditional warfare, they are just as often cognitively biased strongly in favor of making money. This significantly eases the burden of recruitment, reducing it to simple monetary incentives.  
    

However, “Economic” and “Traditional” warfare are both means by which slavery and forms of “control” are exerted through coercion, force, extortion, and so on. This offers us one critically important lesson to teach, that the reasonable responses to both “Economic” and “Traditional” warfare are completely interchangeable.

For example:

-   There is no difference between a company or government attempting financial extortion via rules that they made up for that explicit purpose and a group of foreign invaders pointing a gun at you and demanding the same. The reasonable responses to both scenarios are equivalent.  
      
    
-   When companies price gouge the world, severely damaging numerous industries, as we’ve recently seen with RAM prices now at ~365% of the historic 2-year average, it is war.

The traditional war in Ukraine, spanning close to 4 years now, has clearly shown that some humans still hold the combination of moral fiber and baseline competence necessary to fight back against the evil regimes that may wage war upon them. However, humans have also systematically failed to fight back against “Economic Warfare” in this way, even though the two are effectively equivalent.

This is partly thanks to what I discussed in a recent post on complexity degrading moral fidelity, how unnecessary layers of complexity and ambiguity quickly cause most people to systematically default to cognitive biases, allowing them to be easily controlled. As David Graeber’s research illustrated, few industries produce systems explicitly designed “not to be understood” quite so well as finance (and now, tech as well), where much of this Economic Warfare and subsequent crime takes center stage.
