The Tech Industry status at the end of 2025, in a nutshell:

• AWS and Azure have hoarded GPUs and related hardware, to such a degree that they can’t plug much of it in, lacking sufficient raw electrical power, creating “Zombie” facilities as AWS employees nicknamed them.

\*Microsoft’s CEO also noted this, bragging that they have more hardware than they can plug in.

• The US doesn’t even come close to having enough electrical power (or available water supply in many local areas) to handle current demand, let alone the unhinged and blatantly absurd “plans” that AWS, Microsoft, OpenAI, Oracle, Nvidia, and other tech companies are peddling to inflate their stock prices.

The US as a whole has “Grade D+” energy infrastructure (https://infrastructurereportcard.org/cat-item/energy-infrastructure/), and the electrical grid in particular is over 20 years past its expiration date. These problems might take a decade to remedy, requiring many trillions in infrastructure investment beyond anything already made. Energy sector investments have also been torched by the current ruling regime, setting this back even further, attempting to undermine previous infrastructure investment. Also note, the median time to construct a new nuclear reactor is ~121 months (nearly 11 years): https://www.statista.com/statistics/712841/median-construction-time-for-reactors-since-1981/

• Though the statistics have since become a search hazard, it was previously reported that as much as 85% of AWS’s GPUs for rent were sitting idle on the average day, meaning that demand for all the hardware that they already have running simply isn’t there. They are hoarding something where actual usage is already abysmal.

• Ordinary citizens globally are currently being price-gouged to the tune of ~250% above the previous stable value of RAM, which has caused the rapid decline of virtually every part of the hardware industry, and those industries dependent on it, including literal “shrinkflation” on the specs of all hardware, even as prices continue to rise.

This, of course, is directly due to those same tech companies bragging about hoarding more hardware than they can currently use, and which they won’t be able to use any time soon either. Even paying a 50% higher price on electricity in some regions, they can’t buy energy that doesn’t exist. They can also realistically only push “consumers” so far in a country where automatic weapons are both cheap and readily available.

In summary, the tech industry is hoarding hardware that it can’t use today, and won’t be able to use any time soon, with the deployed share of that same hardware mostly sitting idle right now. All of this, while they directly harm nearly every other industry, pushing circular investment schemes to the limit.
