For the past 3.5 years people have been acutely concerned with “Hallucination” in AI models (the technical term is “BS”), but as I listen to podcasts with people like Sam Harris talking about AI today, I’m growing more concerned with the AI-fraud-induced-psychosis among podcast hosts and guests.

The “model collapse” issue with LLMs and their derivatives is highly similar in system dynamics terms to what we’re observing among such “influencers” and “experts”, where they speak as though they had some technical understanding, optimized for plausibility, but the things that they say clearly mark a departure from any such understanding. Much like models degenerate under an increasing load of BS, humans do the same for any such content that they fail to reject, albeit to a reduced degree.

Part of this problem stems from polarization operating within hyper-complexity, as cognitive biases and social mechanisms drive people to simplify and cluster, orienting themselves around equally simplified and clustered motivations. This leads different people to cluster around social groups with vaguely compatible motivations, and by accepting those social clusters all of the malevolent pathogens contained therein gain purchase within their minds.

This also produces another distinct problem as polarization matures and factions drift further apart. Beyond a certain point, each polarized faction begins to treat anything or anyone residing at the neutral point between them as though it belonged to an opposing faction. This further heavily de-incentivizes neutrality, as it causes any neutral parties to be marked as an enemy by all sufficiently polarized factions.

The issue also compounds further with the “cognitive offloading”, where any higher cognitive activity is delegated elsewhere. In the “e/acc” faction of AI this offloading is directed at AI models, and in the opposing polarized faction it is frequently directed to infected bobbleheads who’re treated as “influencers” and “experts”.

In the true Soviet spirit of Lenin, this is an example of his tactic of “controlling the opposition”, where both polarized factions are controlled by a single party. It also makes both factions a complete laughing stock to anyone sitting in the neutral middle, further increasing the incentive to demonize the absence of polarization, from both sides.

Any entity or group that acutely fails to understand that which they obsess over and act upon builds a foundation for failure, frequently both systematic and fairly predictable. This degrades their forecasting abilities to that of a “broken clock”, where they might be right twice per day, but that is the limit of it. Consequently, all of their talk about “the future” becomes no more than increasingly unhinged fantasy.

The incentive structures of social media, many corporations, and the social dynamics of many groups more generally discourage the hard road of recovery from pathogenic and pathological floods of disinformation. This is greatly reinforced by financial incentives from both polarized sides, cultivating dependencies and fears.

Don’t bet on those who’ve fallen recovering from it any time soon.
