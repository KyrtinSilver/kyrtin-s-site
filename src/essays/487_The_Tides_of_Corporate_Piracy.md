“A rising tide raises all boats”, including pirate ships.

In many ways, this describes the past several years, where pirates like Nvidia, Microsoft, OpenAI, and Anthropic (among others currently embroiled in countless court cases for said piracy) have been riding a high tide of malevolent money, like the “privateers” of past centuries.

The trick is what happens when the tide goes back out.

We’ve already seen the start of it, such as companies being herded into “Defense” industry contracts with very “flexible” terms (for the US government), one of the only industries with both money to burn and enough fools to burn it. The “No honor among thieves” saying comes to mind, as those who exploited countless others may predictably be exploited by other bad actors during such low tides.

As virtually everyone with a head on their shoulders expected, the introduction of “advertising” into “services” like ChatGPT caused a massive backlash and a major drop in their ability to convert free users to paid subscribers, the latter of which they still lose money on anyway, even after the kind of financial “book cooking” that lands someone in a prison cell next to Sam Bankman-Fried.

The other tech giants have faired no better, burning money on the few who pay them a fraction of what it costs them, while also burning it on free offerings and failing miserably to bring in paid subscriptions. It never made any financial sense, and it still doesn’t for that dumpster fire of transformer-based tech.

When all of the pirate ships are beached and broken, the rum all gone, their former denizens being devoured by hoards of sea crabs with the face of Gary Marcus, then the funds that were wasted on them will flow somewhere else.

If that new flow is directed by the LLM-brained (brain-rotted) among humanity, then I wouldn’t place any bets on human survival, but as Iceland famously demonstrated in response to a far less severe financial crisis, the guilty parties may all be sent to prison. A path away from piracy may be the next frontier, if people so choose.

Just as the next frontier doesn’t need to cultivate piracy, nor does it need to leave copyright law in the sad degenerate state that it stands in today. Turning usage into fair, dynamic, combinatorial, and automated compensation is a hyper-complex problem, but one that different technology, already demonstrated, may be applied to solving.

The previous wave of “bullshitting machines” AI (as termed in the “ChatGPT is Bullshit” paper) aren’t retrieval systems, nor are they cumulative, aligned, or bound to reality. The frontiers that may see investment in the coming months and years can be, but the pirates of the past several years will find no shelter there.

\*\*Note, yes I’m implying that in the event of shipwrecks, the monkey may be eaten first.
