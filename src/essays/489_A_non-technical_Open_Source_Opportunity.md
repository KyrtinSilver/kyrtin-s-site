There is a non-technical opportunity to contribute to the current Open Source project, focused on one method of integrating human knowledge and perspective into building more capable and better aligned systems, collective intelligence.

In this case, it focuses on the seed material that a system’s graph grows out of, via a sort of associative exercise, and one or more kinds of data that may be contributed to any node within the graph.

The easiest of these potential contributions fall into two categories, “metadata”, which are functionally equivalent to words and phrases serving like hashtags, as well as “related goals”, which is precisely what it sounds like.

In this case, metadata serves to shape and grow the connectome of a graph structure, with related goals serving a similar, but more limited function. They both serve as part of the skeletal knowledge structure that a system first comes online with and iterates from.

Other non-technical contributions can take the form of any Open Source compatible and on-topic materials for Cybersecurity, Medicine, or more philosophy and governance oriented materials, with an emphasis on high-quality peer-reviewed materials (preferably pre-GenAI) utilizing academic citations.

Following phase 1, semi-technical contributions could also take the form of groups of people contributing their perspectives to the motivational system and associated graph data, but those do require some degree of specialized knowledge to translate an individual’s actual perspective into the intended simplified structure.
