Honesty with a big stick is (virtually) always the best (long-term) policy. It is partly the lack of a big stick or the belief that one isn’t necessary where people tend to go wrong with honesty.

That said, it is highly subject to the “Order of Operation”, meaning that the order in which you carry out the process is often critical to the achievement of any desirable result. This is also where most people tend to struggle, as the complexity of choosing an optimal path through this sequential ordering often explodes into an incalculable mess of possible scenarios.

Beyond even the optimal Order of Operation, you run into the matter of granularity/quality in the delivery of each step and transitions between them. This takes a process that is likely already hyper-complex and causes the number of possible scenarios to explode even further.

Since this far exceeds the capacities of human cognition, then it is navigated by Cognitive Biases, with the best results being biases that have been conditioned according to extensive experience in handling similar problems before. The “10,000 hours of practice for the mastery of a skill” figure is one example of conditioning Cognitive Biases over time in this manner, allowing them to navigate hyper-complexity as well as current human limitations may allow.

Honesty relates to all of this by designating a specific (and very small) subset of the overall search space, functionally serving like a Cognitive Bias by reducing the overall complexity of the problem, while allowing other cognitive biases to operate within it. Like Cognitive Biases, it also holds strong advantages, such as requiring no separate memory storage for “What parties X, Y, and Z were led to believe.”, allowing for a much simpler mask of memory declaring what has been told already, for Order of Operation purposes.

Generally, honesty offers multiple paths to favorable outcomes in both mid and long-term when applied over any subject matter, provided that the Order of Operation is carefully selected and ample effort (and/or experience) are applied to the granular quality of delivery for that path.

The “big stick” functions as both a deterrent (Like Mutually Assured Destruction, M.A.D.) , as well as a means of dealing with the long-tail of probability, where rare and potent events occur. Neglect of this both raises risks by losing the deterrent, as well as failing mitigation for such events. Like operating a facility with no safety equipment, sooner or later the lack of it will backfire.

Much as Honesty offers these advantages, building your thoughts and communication around scientifically demonstrated and/or proven materials (avoiding opinions and beliefs) offers many of the same advantages, and a few additional ones. That is a much harder thing to deliver in practice due to the added complexity, but even the effort toward that end is well worthwhile.
