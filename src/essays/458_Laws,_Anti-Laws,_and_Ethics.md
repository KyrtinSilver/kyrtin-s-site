The only “laws” that may be said to exist are those which are consistently enforced. This fact was acknowledged more than 800 years ago with the Magna Carta. I’ll term the inconsistently enforced subset as “anti-laws”, which no one may be compelled to abide by.

Likewise, “laws” constitute a social compact among the people of a given group or region, as they have little or nothing to do with “morals”, and exponentially less to do with “ethics”. This means that no “authority” (technically a cognitive bias) figure may create or alter the enforcement of “laws” without the confirmed support of those impacted by the decision.

Someone who attempts to exploit the inconsistency of “anti-laws” is quite plainly a “criminal”. When such a person places themselves, or is placed, in a position of “authority” where they dictate new “laws” (and anti-laws) as well as how “anti-laws” may be enforced, they become a “dictator”.

Some models of governance systematize and dilute this form of corruption by selectively injecting “religion” (social, in-group) in place of “cultural morals” (social, all-group), adding additional layers of complexity and obfuscation, to much the same effect as a dictator. Many dictators also latch onto these systematic vulnerabilities, allowing for much easier production of “demagogues”.

The wealthy of most countries worldwide exploit anti-laws through the carrot & stick of wealth that they hold, an advantage not accessible to >99% of the population. However, everyone else has access to the stick, and the size of that stick grows with the portion of the population who take hold of it.

A simple historic example of this is the idea of “unions”, or strength in numbers. One critical and often neglected factor determining the influence of that stick is the willingness to actually use it. You can “walk softly, but carry a big stick” to strong effect, but only so long as others know that you’re fully willing and prepared to swing it.

Ethics, the hypothetical point where cognitive bias has been removed from moral systems (which individual humans may only approximate), is what separates someone violating fundamentally arbitrary and transient “laws” and “anti-laws” from “dictators” and “demagogues”. Under ethics, personal gains may be one by-product among many, but for dictators and demagogues they are the point.

Ethics is also achieved through systems of collective intelligence, which by definition reduce cognitive biases, meaning that the same governance structures used to create laws are utilized to reach ethics. This means that while one individual approximating ethics may violate “laws” and “anti-laws” that weren’t created through such collective intelligence, that same individual is subject to the laws of any such collective intelligence system which they are a member of.

Lastly, membership within any group is fundamentally chosen by the individual, it may never be given through “birth” or “law”, as that would violate autonomy.
