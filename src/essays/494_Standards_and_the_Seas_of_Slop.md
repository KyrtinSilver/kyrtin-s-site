Standards are a trickier topic than people think. You can have high or low standards, as well as increasing or decreasing standards, with every change inserting an opportunity for maladaptation, where adaptation causes systematically wrong results.

The torrential flood of Slop across the internet has kept some groups not only fed, but gorged, to the point where they may consume little else. This has become fairly common, taking many forms, and supercharging both new and old forms of maladaptive behavior, from AI-induced psychosis to “AI boyfriend/girlfriend” puppetry.

However, the opposite exists too, where instead of low and decreasing standards people choose to increase their own standards via exposure. This means that an individual may be exposed to the best 3 or 4 examples of a given type of system, creating standards where anything competing for the same must meet or exceed them.

In dynamic systems where an increasing standard operates, this can quickly reduce an overwhelming volume of options down to a truly tiny number to choose from, avoiding choice overload and face-stomping the malevolence of marketing into the ground.

There is just one problem. If the overall system is degenerating, such as systems being flooded with slop, then the number of increasing-standards options can easily drop to zero, and remain at zero.

Human fiction, spanning many mediums, news reporting, government operations, supply chains, and countless other similar systems are all slowly circling the drain of this degeneration, and have been for some years. The motion is harder to spot when almost everything is moving to the same, with only narratives serving up illusions of progress.

Personally, I’ve nearly run out of media or activities that meet my standards, as I’ve continued my climb, watching the rest go down the drain. While the lowest forms of life on earth gorge themselves on AI Slop I see only a barren wasteland, already post-apocalyptic on the digital front, with war offering a segway into an analog implementation of the same.

Counter-intuitively, looking out across the wasteland of the now is not a cause for depression, but rather disappointment in humanity, and disgust. It brings to mind a quote “Immortality isn’t living forever. That’s not what it feels like. Immortality is everybody else dying.”

You are what you consume, and I would choose this wasteland over a world of delusional slop any day. Anything worth doing tends to require walking “...in all the empty places…”. Only time may tell of the destination, but there is value in the journey regardless.

Whatever the chances of success may be in walking such empty places, the chances of success for the alternative are precisely zero, and thus infinitely lower.
