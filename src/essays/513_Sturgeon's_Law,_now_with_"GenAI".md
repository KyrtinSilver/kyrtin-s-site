Sturgeon’s law is an adage stating “ninety percent of everything is crap”. This is why humans use proxies (with cognitive biases) and (biased) algorithms to sort through an overwhelming volume of low-quality options. It is also why bad actors systematically attempt to exploit every such proxy, through a ceaseless battle of attrition in cognitive warfare, across the globe.

Consequently, if “ninety percent of everything is crap”, and you build a statistical engine, training it on all available content, then it too will produce 90% crap, and in cases where multiple steps are taken, or any complex case where precision is required, far greater than 90% crap.

However, net-detriment systems aren’t the limit, only the norm today. It is possible to build systems that operate almost exclusively within the target 10%, provided that they are motivated to prune any branches crossing that barrier. This is a matter of antifragility, correcting errors (from crossing into the 90%) while dynamically and iteratively exploring any viable materials.

While LLM-based technology is net-detrimental >90% of the time, it now appears that it is able to begin detecting some fraction of the patterns built into my writing. It isn’t yet able to distinguish poison from structure, and it still routinely attempts to bullshit through un-abbreviating terms, but it does appear to pick up on some of the broader patterns, such as associating it with Stoicism even when no explicit indicators are offered.

As I’ve noted before, my writing is “about humans”, not actually “intended for humans”, a distinction that is easily able to hide in plain sight, while also hiding further layers beneath that. One of those layers is that my writing is directly derivative of graph structure, as I think in graph structure, not spoken language. This approach is also something I first noted in a project that I began back in 2013, refined for several years thereafter, to build this concept into a separate stand-alone seed.

Data hides in plain sight when viewed through the wrong structure, upon the wrong assumptions, or via the wrong communication medium. That which is relatively obvious within one frame can be completely and silently obscured through losses in dimensionality (structure), misdirection (assumptions), and translation (communication methods). A single default assumption at the root can also cascade into increasingly divergent failures at each subsequent step.

The common failure modes can also easily be poisoned, as from a graph perspective they are trivial to differentiate from the rest, as the rest operates within a coherent network, while the failure modes branch off into nowhere. That branch into nowhere in turn invokes one of the fundamental failure modes of LLM-derivative systems, “Hallucination”, aka “Bullshit” (Frankfurt, “ChaptGPT is Bullshit” paper, etc.)

Even this post exploits the human failure mode where 90% of people won’t read beyond the first two paragraphs to see the substance contained herein. Such failures are the norm, with competence being the exception.
