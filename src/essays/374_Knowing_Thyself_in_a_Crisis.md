The phrase translating to “Know Thyself” is well-known, but part of living up to that in practice involves several critical steps, applicable to both business and life in general:

1\. Draw your lines in the sand well in advance, and only allow them to be redrawn at pre-defined events or intervals. This prevents drift and manipulation.

2\. Define your “Scorched Earth” scenario, the point beyond which your quality of life, KPIs, or other metrics cease to matter, where the “greater good” is prioritized. I sometimes half-jokingly refer to this as going “Full Frank Castle” (aka “The Punisher”). As the half-joke and term imply, all nuclear options are on the table under this scenario.

3\. If your line in the sand is crossed, map all roads to Scorched Earth. To navigate through difficulty, you need to know which roads do and do not lead to that scenario. That scenario is generally to be avoided, but if it can’t be avoided, you also need to know that to be your destination in advance.

4\. Give no warning. If dealing with hostilities, where a line has been crossed and some roads lead to Scorched Earth, give the offending party no warning of the consequences that await them should that scenario become necessary. This is difficult, because humans extract more satisfaction from saying what they’ll do than actually doing it, making it a matter of discipline.

Numerous philosophers have said something to the effect that “it is the measure of a person how they respond to a crisis”, and crisis response and damage mitigation reliably plays out better with such early process planning (drawing lines), worst-case planning (Scorched Earth), mapping of processes to possible outcomes (roads to Scorched Earth), and the discipline of acting according to strict tactics and strategy, rather than emotional satisfaction.

“Live that way, and nothing will surprise you. Everything that happens will be something that you’ve seen before.”

If you draw no lines in the sand, define no Scorched Earth, and give warnings to hostiles, then you’re unfit to lead or manage much of anything. You shouldn’t aim for Scorched Earth, but if lines are crossed and all roads remaining to you lead there, then the worst scenario isn’t reaching Scorched Earth, it is reaching it unprepared.
