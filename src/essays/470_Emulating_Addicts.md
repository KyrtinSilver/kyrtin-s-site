One of the key architectural failures of popular Artificial (Un)Intelligence is that it is both devoid of any real motivational system, and trained primarily on data from those humans whose motivation has been drained away, leaving little more than highly neurotic and internet-addicted husks.

Those highly addicted and neurotic husks are also the most common high-volume users of said technology, often reflecting their own neuroticisms back to them with added sycophancy, as well as being designed to maximize anthropomorphism. This has led to no shortage of increasingly deranged masses developing emotional attachments to their own fictional and sycophantic AI-generated reflections, a new kind of narcissism.

While people can and have argued for emulating humans via training such relatively simple transformer-based models, you can’t even build a shallow emulation of human motivation when the humans that the training data derives from “live” in a near-zero motivational state the overwhelming majority of their data-generating time.

At best, such training only amounts to a low-dimensionality shadow puppet of how human motivation works. In practice, training on motivationally drained humans, such as those doom-scrolling social media or mindlessly surfing YouTube, means that only a shallow emulation of those operating absent higher cognition may be achieved. In other words, you get a low-quality emulation of cognitive biases, and little else.

This problem is further compounded by those same cognitively-absent and motivationally drained humans producing orders of magnitude more AI Slop than their cognitively engaged counterparts. When Slop is positioned as the low-hanging fruit, the motivationally drained consume it in massive quantities, to the exclusion of virtually all else.

This cycle of Slop generation and consumption further degrades all non-frozen or new models, biasing them in favor of it by volume and probability. Recommender algorithms further compound this issue.

One of the predictable side-effects of any “attention economy” is the mass-degradation of higher cognition. Less higher cognition translates into “more effective marketing” and “more (mindless) engagement”, satisfying the narrow KPIs that many algorithms are built to seek out.

Training Artificial (Un)Intelligence on the data resulting from that mass-decline of cognition anchors it to the same patterns, while adding new forms of degeneration that are tied to the model architecture. The resulting system compounds one type of decline upon the other, making it doubly-degenerative.

While transformer-based technology isn’t capable of being mentally ill, the people generating and consuming the majority of AI Slop are certainly both capable and delivering on that capability. The technology is trained on that mental illness, increasing in both volume and severity, sending it and those using it most down the drain.
