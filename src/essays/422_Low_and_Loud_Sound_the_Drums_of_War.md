For a long time now, the argument for China not to invade Taiwan has been the severe disruption to silicon that would follow such an invasion. Well, that argument is dead now, the Tsunami of snake oil from AI has severely disrupted silicon.

Other geopolitical hot-spots are also home to parts of the same supply chain, and by the protective value of one point in the supply chain evaporating, they all do. Take South Korea, for example, home to Samsung.

What people thinking in narrow economic terms of pricing and stock values tend to forget is that there are no rules, no authority, and no actual monopoly on violence. For example, let’s do a little broader math, putting this train wreck into figures and alternatives:

The price of RAM has already gone up to 3x, and indications are that it’ll keep going higher in 2026, maybe even through 2028, which led a variety of bad actors to begin hoarding it, besides the biggest bad actor, OpenAI, who very intentionally started this war.

Here is the litmus test, in pseudo-code:

Current (Extortion) Rate of RAM – Actual Value Price = Alternative Investment Margin (AIM)

IF “AIM” > “Negligible cost Margin” THEN “Hostile targets” is appended to the metadata of “Guilty Parties”.

This means that competent individuals and companies who are unwilling to get more than negligibly screwed over by fraud won’t take the bait, they’ll save that money and may invest it in more productive ways. It also means that the difference between a 3x price hike on RAM and a 1,000,000x price hike is virtually nonexistent, both are completely unacceptable, and the latter only serves to compel the following action:

IF “AIM” < “Cost of Weapons” THEN “War” is appended to the array of options on the table.

This is true of the decisions of individuals, as well as those of nations. A hundred people could very well go full Frank Castle on OpenAI’s employees, but the more likely scenario is that with one of the main reasons not to invade Taiwan and other geopolitical targets now thoroughly destroyed, the odds of war dramatically increase.

Yes, War has been an option for thousands of years, and such means have always been an option when dealing with frauds and other bad actors.

To bring this formula home: 64 GB of DDR5 went from ~$200 to over $700, making the AIM over $500. The price of new weapons, including automatic AR-15s, is less than $500, as shown in the attached screenshot.

War is now an option on a lot of tables. It became one the moment prices hit 3x. Merry Christmas, OpenAI took history’s most massive bubble, and made it a war.
