One of the rare individuals with a talent for eloquence in discussing the tech industry’s hardware side is Steve Burke, Editor-In-Chief of Gamers Nexus. When it comes to his coverage of the completely artificial (price fixing) hike on DRAM, I couldn’t have said it better myself: [WTF Just Happened? | The Corrupt Memory Industry & Micron](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9A-eeJP0J7c)

For anyone wondering “WTF” with respect to computer hardware, I can give his coverage of these topics my highest recommendation. He covers the hard figures, why they change, how they compare over time, and what it all means.

The computer, phone, and any other kind of “smart” hardware market for consumers is being rapidly priced-out. There is no silver lining to this, and if such a Cartel is allowed to operate in this way without consequence, others will undoubtedly follow. This has happened before.

As he points out, this is very much a larger industry scam taking aim at forcing people to buy substantially crappier hardware, locking them in to pay for cloud services to handle the things that their hardware can no longer handle. Those circular “investments”, besides being outright illegal, clearly paint the incentive structure backing this activity, encouraging the capture of consumers into ever-more confined human factory farms.

Equally predictable, and Dystopian, is that if such Cartels are successful, and these nefarious efforts expanded, then they could indeed force people to use “cloud” services for things that they should have been perfectly able to do locally on their own hardware, and then they could jack up the price of those services by 10x.

As he also notes, we saw much milder versions of this with things like Adobe’s “Creative Cloud”, which there was never any reason to turn into a subscription service to begin with, except for a malevolent cash-grab. The same is true of Microsoft Office, simple software without the faintest shred of an excuse to pay a monthly fee for it.  
  
Those companies got away with it, but if the entire DRAM and broader Silicon industry gets away with this one, then the consequences may be global, severe, and lasting.

This particular activity also reeks to the highest degree, as the cutting edge of technology uses DRAM, not brute-force GPU paperweights, but the price isn’t being increased because of that, just the opposite. The price is being increased under the pretense of pandering to LLM-based trashbot technology, an extremely far cry from anything cutting edge. This increase, besides being a Cartel’s cash-grab, directly harms that cutting edge, while playing Weekend at Bernie’s with the dead-end of LLM-based tech.

This all begs the question, when will the guillotine drop?
