In the Cognitive Bias and Psychology literature, both research and pop culture, many common tricks of manipulation are mentioned, such as “Cold Reading”, which strongly relates to both AI and Tarot cards.

“Cold readings commonly employ high-probability guesses, quickly picking up on signals as to whether their guesses are in the right direction or not. The reader then emphasizes and reinforces any accurate connections while quickly moving on from missed guesses. Psychologists believe that this appears to work because of the Barnum effect and due to confirmation biases within people.”

Sound familiar? You can find similar dynamics in people using Tarot cards, or for that matter, interacting with LLM-based systems.

With Tarot cards, people reveal a card, integrate it into some ongoing mental narrative, quietly distorting it to fit their own narrative and circumstances, and they iterate, doing the same for one card after another. The result mirrors the dynamics of a sort of social and cognitive game, storytelling, often with deeply personal narratives in play.

With LLM-based systems (glorified chatbots), people often prompt the system with much the same intention as the Tarot cards, a social and cognitive mental exercise or game, where they build up a mental narrative with each response, adding their own distortions each step of the way. This is further compounded by the LLM-based systems being architected, trained, and optimized to seek out high-probability guesses given arbitrary text, some of which is literally derived from humans using “Cold Reading” techniques.

The limitation of Tarot cards and similar historical examples is that they have a limited number of cards and combinations, as well as the strong asymmetry of input for the interaction. This translates to more cognitive labor and less tailored/emotionally satisfying results. LLM-based systems utilize both push and pull for that same input, while being able to “generate” any arbitrary equivalent of a card that might play into a developing narrative. As that process “generates”, rather than utilizing a fixed and limited library, it is also able to optimize (and opportunistically manipulate) as the process unfolds, just like Cold Reading.

Again, LLM-based systems are architected, trained, and optimized to seek out those high-probability guesses, iteratively predicting token after token, in response after response. They home in on probabilities revealed within each interaction, even though the process is context-blind, as they emulate the sum of human interactions from scraped web data, as those humans fell prey to the Barnum Effect and Confirmation Bias in large numbers.

Marketing manages to sucker some people into this, that is why the field of marketing exists as it does today, or arguably for that matter, why it exists at all. Enron Musk, Mark Suckerberg and Scam Altman have all reached where they are through the heavy and successful exploitation of many human cognitive biases at scale, just as the technology that they peddle does today.

Why do humans fall for it? The same reasons that they play with Tarot cards and fortune-tellers.
