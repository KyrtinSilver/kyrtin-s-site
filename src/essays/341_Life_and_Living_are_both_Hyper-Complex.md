“Hyper-Complexity” is one of my favorite subjects, as well as an easy way to clear a room, as many people instinctively flee at the mention of it. It is the region of complexity where no individual or group of humans is capable of effectively handling that complexity, using any combination of tools or methods currently at their disposal.

Human Cognitive Biases exist as a potent and large toolbox of means for coping with this, albeit arbitrarily poorly, and progressively worse as that complexity increases. They are utilized fluidly, in countless combinations, and automatically, unconsciously, further reducing cognitive load.

This has allowed humanity to survive across pre-history and up to the modern day, but as the complexity of global human society has exponentially increased humanity’s handling of that same complexity has moved almost exponentially in the opposite direction, the predictable result of which is extinction, if left to the status quo. “GenAI” only accelerates this, potentially making it doubly-exponential in decline, as it mirrors the worst elements of Cognitive Bias being automated, without the contextual understanding to ground processes.

Some common examples of Hyper-complexity that virtually every person on the planet faces include choosing:

-   Where to live: For many people the scope of possible choices might include a large portion of the planet, and though they might each have a dozen personal priorities, there may be a hundred different factors influencing those personalized dozen. Apply all factors at a global scale, or even a city or country-level scale, and you have hyper-complexity to one degree or another.  
      
      
    
-   Who to live and form relationships with: Whether for friends, romance, or simply economically viable roommates, at least the same number of factors influence this decision (often more) as choosing where to live, and you have around 8 billion potentials to choose from.  
      
    
-   How to live your life: The open-world sandbox that is life offers little intelligent (non-malevolent) guidance toward what people should do for a career, hobby, or in their spare time. There are no tutorials for life, let alone finding a personalized fit, and an overwhelming abundance of bad actors seek to manipulate people attempting to face this choice. This choice is especially hyper-complex, due to the even broader attack surface for bad actors, as well as how it ties to many more such choices.  
      
    
-   What to eat: Yes, even the choice of what to have for dinner is hyper-complex. You have a grocery store full of potential ingredients (not to mention restaurants), millions of recipes and their variations, as well as whatever you know about your own health, and all that you probably don’t know about medicine, biochemistry, genetics, and microbiomes.
    

  
If you want solutions to hyper-complexity, the technology that my team works with is usually going to be a hard requirement.
