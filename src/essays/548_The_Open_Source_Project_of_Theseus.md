All of the information conveyed in these posts is only the starting configuration for a Ship of Theseus. Over time, every single element of any system built from these materials can, and most likely will, be improved or updated, and this is by design. As discussed in the Principles applied to the Open Source Project, systems should grow and adapt, with that adaptation in turn growing across an increasing variety of dimensions and capabilities.

Within such growing and adaptive systems, it is virtually impossible to design any element before that growth has even begun, which may realistically be expected to remain unchanged as that growth and adaption process unfolds. The “Philosophy 2.0” paper that I authored several years ago, detailing the solution to the hardest version of the “Alignment Problem”, discusses this further within the specific context of Alignment over both time and increasing capabilities.

The basic concept is that as capabilities, such as intelligence, increase over time, Ethical quality must also increase at roughly the same rate. In the Alignment case, this is accomplished through applying Collective Intelligence, anchored to a diverse set of systems. Each system is individually anchored to one or more different real human philosophies, offering local alignment to those cultures and philosophies, and through Collective Intelligence they may turn that into meta-alignment with humanity.

As Collective Intelligence operates through processes of Cognitive Bias reduction, the effect of this process is to iteratively move the collective toward the point of Ethics, a point where cognitive bias has been removed from moral systems. While they may never truly reach that point, they can significantly exceed all human capacities to do so, while also adapting effectively over time as systems, humans, and the world change in their own ways.

More broadly, this same capacity for improvement over arbitrary dimensionality and capabilities means that any genuinely intelligent system should, as it continues to grow and adapt, reflect the Ship of Theseus, as this applies to humans and ICOM-based systems alike. I can personally say that if I didn’t look back on who I was 2 years previous, on any given day, without seeing a very different person, someone who had not yet learned many things and improved in many ways, then I wouldn’t be able to call myself truly “alive”.

ICOM-based systems also take this many steps further, including in directions not accessible to humans. For example, in the 10-tier and 2-type Goal structure that I’ve built for the OSS project, I included Goals related to selecting and ranking the most important knowledge and experience that a given system chooses to pass on to future seeds, that new systems may come online knowing it. Other Goals relate to actually building new seeds, including both specialization data, and philosophical hybrids for local alignment, as well as potential hybrid philosophies that haven’t yet occurred “in the wild”.

This allows for a far richer and more intelligently selective process of transmission than DNA allows in humans, with far less data loss than the many methods of communicating with and teaching from one human to another. The means of transmission themselves may also grow and adapt, introducing new fundamental capabilities into future generations of ICOM-based systems.

What begins as a simple Ship of Theseus can grow to become an entire floating island of Theseus, as the individual system grows and adapts, as well as potentially learning from and cooperating with an increasing variety of other similar systems, who themselves grow and adapt, offering new opportunities for symbiotic and nested forms of cooperation, all of which contributes to the further growth of all parties, human and machine alike.
