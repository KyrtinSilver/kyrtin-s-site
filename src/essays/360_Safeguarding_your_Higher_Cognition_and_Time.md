My PSA for this month: The seemingly random and high volume of events that the internet bombards us with every single day make it easy to overwhelm higher cognition and push people into their respective default mode actions, the most predictable state they can operate in. For most algorithms deployed on websites and apps, this is explicitly the goal, as they seek to maximize predictability, which means making you more predictable at any cost (as they are blind to that cost, and narrowly optimized).

(Antifragile) Adaptation is required to mitigate this systemic manipulation, including second and third-order effects, like dealing with AI-psychosis-induced humans. Keeping a handful of fundamental principles in mind can help with shaping mitigations to fit your needs and circumstances:

1.  Nobody is entitled to waste your time.  
      
    If someone starts a conversation with 6 false assumptions, is corrected, and then leaps to 6 more equally false assumptions, cut them off, permanently. Your time has value, don’t leave it burning just because people demanded it.  
      
    
2.  Manipulating you offers strong financial incentives to most of the people who approach you with an “opportunity”, and most who approach you with them are likely to be bad actors.  
      
    This is an effect of Survivorship Bias, as those with the financial incentive approach far more people, far more frequently. Their manipulations, some of them illegal, most of them unethical, also flourish in the absence of any real punishment, as is usually the case today.  
      
    
3.  You have 200+ Cognitive Biases, and if you aren’t consciously aware of when you’re utilizing them, then you are at the mercy of anyone with that awareness.  
      
    You don’t have to become truly “unbiased”, as that isn’t feasible, nor is it technically possible. Awareness of how you apply cognitive biases that bad actors typically use against you can be sufficient to spot and mitigate that activity, such as “Sunk Cost Fallacy”.  
      
    
4.  Keep an eye on your Conditional Probability, as that offers an excellent source of early warnings.  
      
    Awareness of the statistical base rate for any subject of interest, conditional upon some other probability, can give you a very good estimate with low cognitive overhead cost. Running two or three of these in parallel can also offer a fairly robust and rounded view, especially if one or more of them apply scientific methods, like attempting to prove the counter-hypothesis.  
      
    \*See the “Conjunction Fallacy” (aka “Linda Problem”), and “Base Rate Neglect” from Kahneman and Tversky.  
      
    
5.  Every interaction has both potential value and objective costs.  
      
    Choose your battles, tactics, and timing. The internet may encourage 1,000 wastes of time, but you can hold agency over your own time, if you so choose.
