Ironically, something that a great many people in pre-funding Startups seem to forget is that they don’t have any money. I can say this with certainty, because if people consistently remembered this rather obvious fact, then there wouldn’t be such persistent and permissive predatory middlemen pretending to be “investors” or \[investor-adjacent\].

A startup that falls for such garbage might get funded in the short-term (often on terrible terms, like the over 40% equity lost in the laughingstock of Mistral’s seed round), but it will be far more likely to fail in the long-term for the same reason, “A sucker and their funds are easily parted”, which will remain that much more true once they have more funds to be suckered out of.

Many, if not most, real investors seem to be the “Hikkokomori” (引きこもり) of the financial world, never stepping outside of their (often sycophantic) little bubbles, excluding when those bubbles periodically burst. Perhaps “Fincel” could be a term for such secluded creatures. (I say this as an introvert)

Fincels like those who invested in OpenAI, Anthropic, and similar meme-worthy Ponzi Schemes (as epitomized by the absurd Nvidia and Oracle circular financial road to nowhere) have been surfing the snake oil for years, but every wave eventually crashes, and each bubble bursts. They’re gambling on not wiping out in the process, but they have no tech to land on, only the sharp edges of broken promises.

Other forms of both (mostly) legal and illegal scamming have flourished in this environment that they’ve cultivated, dangling the carrot while fleecing fools of what little (personal) funds they have. Fincels will be Fincels (at least until prison), but you don’t need to fall for scammers while that plays out.

Remember, you don’t have money, you’re seeking funding, and people seeking to extract funds from you rather than those who do the funding either don’t actually know people with funds, or they are too incompetent to persuade them to part with those funds. In either case, such people are scammers, to be avoided, and that’s all that there is to it. See the attached Flow Chart.

Anyone who wants to be permanently blocked and blacklisted can try to evangelize how this isn’t the case with their services aimed at startups, because I’m not taking any of that BS. Competent investors don’t need fancy PowerPoints or pink frilly edges, as plainly evident by the original pitches from numerous high-value companies today.
