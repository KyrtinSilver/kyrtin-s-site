Peace within a mind may not be required for happiness, but it offers a sound foundation upon which to build it. It is fractional, and/or conditional, being a bounded space, but the ability to enter such a state can avoid the predictable pitfalls, like depression.

I wake up, have caffeine and tea and fruit juice, work until I’m hungry, eat something, relax when I need to recover, then work until I can’t, and sleep long and soundly each night. It is a simple routine, but the work is of my own design and choosing, and each need is met. This is peace, not an empty space, nor a Hedonistic one, but one that meets needs, including the need for predictability/stability.

Within this cycle there is no constant escalation, no narrative, and no sense of progression. Those are external, and may be integrated into it, ignored, or a cyclical mix of the two. No coping mechanism or narrative, internalized (identity) or external, can fill an individual without this mental peace.

Such a mental peace provides a safe harbor, an anchor, an origin to return to, and a place to recover. A fierce war may be waged just outside of it, the battle in search of purpose, or against financial/existential insecurities, fighting over narratives and claiming second-hand identities, but within that peaceful mental fortress there is stability, acceptance, routine, recovery, and what little joys each day brings.

I once hated my work, and I walked through hell for many years, knowing depression, and a very long time ago even “fear”, but a trial by fire has its own Population Dynamics. Those who come out of the other side of it intact may heal and grow stronger from it, gaining resistances and immunities along the way, as well as the hardened motivation to prevent others from ever needing to walk that same path again.

It is a lonely path, venturing out into unexplored territory, with no guide, and no companions, because no person may truly walk the path of another.

“No man ever steps in the same river twice, for it’s not the same river and he’s not the same man.”

― Heraclitus

Mental peace is a reward for one’s own progression, choosing your own path and walking it. It can’t be granted by any other, nor may any coping mechanism offer it. It is an easy enough thing for one who holds it to write into code, but most who work in software today have no such peace to offer.

I am not religious or spiritual by any stretch of the imagination, but rather this is practical philosophy, neuroscience, and the predictable products of intentional architecture.

The people who have paddled themselves so far up sh\*^ creek that I may easily point them in the direction back are the same people who do not listen, that is after all usually how they got there in the first place. For virtually all others, none are better equipped to navigate them out of their troubles than they themselves.

I will continue my work, a peaceful mind my internal motivation, as I have done for many years.
