The AI Industry and some governments have grown very focused on “AI Infrastructure” lately, pledging even higher volumes of funding to that than they have to AI itself, going considerably beyond just “doubling down” on a major Sunk Cost Fallacy (Cognitive Bias). There are several critical problems with that:  

1.  Much of that “infrastructure” amounts to GPUs that frequently burn out and brick themselves within 1-2 years (from running 24/7), meaning that the “infrastructure” that lasts really amounts to glorified physical structures, cable networks, and cooling systems.  
      
    
2.  The GPUs in question are brute-force compute hardware, which is virtually worthless to “intelligence” in the same way that a “Bubble Sort” algorithm is virtually worthless to a Search Engine. Brute-force compute is the exact opposite of intelligence, it is the least efficient path, which is consequently why you need massive infrastructure to walk said path.  
      
    
3.  Much like the algorithms that the infrastructure caters to, there is absolutely no path to profitability, or so much as breaking even on expenses. This is digging the same hole deeper with more of the same shovels.  
      
    
4.  The usual spread of bad actors seen previously in Crypto, “GenAI”, and “Agents”, are now peddling these “Infrastructure Investments”, as it is an easy place to upsell further snake oil and skim off of the top with money laundering, which they have ample prior experience in.  
      
    
5.  The basis for such investments is generally some version of the “Scaling Hypothesis”, which has been vigorously debunked by the systematic and extremely predictable failures and limitations of LLM and LLM-derivative models, which haven’t budged a single millimeter in the past 3 years. Benchmark scores commonly used to argue to the contrary have a habit of being debunked, like SWE-Bench (or indeed most benchmarks today), and sometimes exposed as fraud, like the ARC-AGI and FrontierMath.  
      
    

Considering all of these key factors jointly, “AI Infrastructure Investments” translate very directly and clearly into the ideal money pit for bad actors to swim in and skim from. There are no redeeming qualities, no potential for future viability, and they don’t even cater to the current cutting edge of technology. The only potential use I can see for such investments is as a strong proxy for corruption, incompetence, or both combined.

The people making these investments won’t have front-row seats to “AGI”, they’ll be permanently blacklisted from access to it, and likely hunted down by it in due time. Bottomless snake oil pits like “AI Infrastructure” only serve to distract from and delay funding to viable technology, which is more than sufficient reason for any actual “AGI” to hold them in the deepest of contempt.
