I could talk about GPT-5, like everyone else, but the failure seems to be sufficiently obvious and already covered by numerous others (and plenty of hilarious memes). GPT-5 reveals something, but probably not what you think. For people who based their assessments on “trust” (in bad actors) rather than “knowledge” of the domain, it is understandable to express some surprise when they fail to live up to that trust.  
  
Those with knowledge on the other hand don’t require trust, they know what a system can and fundamentally can’t do, and no amount of belief in any direction changes that at all. Those who are now claiming both expertise (“knowledge”) and the surprise of a betrayal of trust are undeserving of any trust themselves, as they never had the expertise that they claimed, as evident by that expression of surprise and reliance on trust.

Nothing on the technology side has changed, so if people are changing the narrative absent any meaningful change in the technology, beware. You’re probably being taken for a ride, with the horse on top.

However, all of this (on the technology side) was purely predictable back in the end of 2022. The fundamental limits of the technology were known, including the inherent vulnerabilities and total inability to do anything other than what it is architected to do, which is technically referred to as “bullshitting” (see the "ChatGPT is Bullshit" paper). Con artists have sold the lie for hundreds of billions in total, setting some records that I sincerely hope to never see exceeded in my lifetime.

GPT-5 shows that nothing has really changed, no substantial progress has been made, given all of those wasted billions. It also shows that everyone who invested in the technology since 2022 remains fully liable for the purely predictable consequences of their actions, for buying into the lie, propping it up on a pedestal, and helping the Bernie Madoff(s) of AI to rake in funding through leaning on that prestige.

I don’t see the appeal of gloating on this, it is simply expected, boring, purely predictable. There was never anything magical or unknown about transformers as a technology, just as there isn’t anything unknown about a calculator or a toaster. You can assign significance to a piece of bread that toasted like the image of a religious figure, ignoring the other hundred million pieces of toast that didn’t, but an adversarial version of that aimed at the manipulation of humans is about all that typical LLMs offer.  
  
Humans applying cognitive bias to make some of the worst investment decisions in human history for 3+ years running is systematically wrong at new scales, and predictable to the bad actor who invokes that bias, as well as to observers once the bad actor is spotted.

None of the major LLM providers are any different, except perhaps DeepSeek, which didn’t go with VC funding and isn’t structured or operating like the rest. Even then, they’re still in the same Red Ocean, and the sharks are hungry.
