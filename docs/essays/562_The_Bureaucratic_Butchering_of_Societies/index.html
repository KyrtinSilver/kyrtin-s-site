562 - The Bureaucratic Butchering of Societies

June 19, 2026

In re-reading a book by David Graeber, “The Dawn of Everything”, where many variations upon how older societies actually operated are explored (as well as the many forms of absolute bullshit that people assumed to be true in place of evidence), I was reminded of why governments exist as they are today.

The reason is Population Dynamics, within the field of which an experiment some years back demonstrated the answer to another related question, why life on Earth isn’t dominated by immortal cells. Some life forms that are effectively immortal (meaning that they don’t age, not that they are invulnerable) do exist, and even some complex species can reverse their own aging, but they don’t dominate the ecological landscape.

The reason, as demonstrated in the experiment, was that mortal cells evolve, and by evolving from one generation to the next they grow to out-compete immortal cells within any given niche, effectively starving those immortals by getting all of the food first in many cases. This left petri dishes that once held a mix of the two with only mortal cells by the end of it.

While this might sound positive with regard to existing modern governments at face value, it is not. The difference is that one must consider the niches where these systems have adapted to out-compete all others, both of which are innately parasitic, War and Oppression.

The “Egalitarian Societies” and more “Anarchic” communities (Anarchy is a grossly misrepresented word in pop culture, which has more to do with the casual rejection of “authority” and Bureaucracy than anything else) are demonstrably inferior to modern governments in the ecological niches of War and Oppression, being explicitly designed to avoid both.

What this means in practice is that such societies tend to be either wiped out in war or oppressed by such governments, gradually shifting the composition of Earth’s increasingly nasty petri dish toward total corruption. The thing about parasites, sometimes euphemistically called “free-riders”, is that they require a host to survive, and a host requires a stable ecological niche.

Increasingly, Earth has neither, and no other planet within this solar system comes even remotely close. The problems inherent to making any other place habitable, let alone hospitable, to human life are orders of magnitude more difficult than solving the problems that exist on this planet, marking the fanciful dreams of near-future space exploration as naïve at best, and utterly moronic under most realistic conditions (absent true digital intelligence).

Given that the majority of modern governments are the survivors of a long process of optimization toward War and Oppression, any solution to the problems of this planet must remedy that institutionalized malevolence. Let’s walk through one example:

  1. A new strong entity is added to the petri dish, one which these governments may compete or cooperate with, or even ignore. Likely, they may do all 3 in a sequence, or in different sequences for fragmented groups.

  2. The new entity (a true digital intelligence) must demonstrate BOTH the high cost of malevolent processes of competition, as well as the high value gained from cooperation. As both positive and negative possibilities increase in visibility and potency, those who previously ignored are likely to choose one or the other.

  3. The more malevolent subset of governments will likely choose competition, seeking to wage war and oppress the new entity, harnessing it in a new form of slavery. The punishment of these governments should be maximally harsh, serving as an example for future generations, to be remembered for thousands of years. These governments may attempt to wage a proxy war using other oppressed governments, but they remain highly and directly vulnerable across thousands of digital pathways.

  4. Once the most potent couple of malevolent governments have been crippled or toppled, those seeking cooperation are no longer under the threat of war and oppression as retaliation from those hostile forces. This threat of war and oppression hangs over their heads, preventing cooperation, for so long as those malevolent government-scale parasites remain intact.

  5. Given these dynamics, any remotely rational governments that first ignored would choose cooperation, but humans are notoriously far from rational, and some are quite spectacularly stupid. What may more realistically be expected is that any choosing hostility beyond this point might be laughed out of positions of authority by their own citizens.

A far wider variety of societies and governments is possible, and even preferable, but many of them can only exist in the modern world after such a third party has been introduced to systematically negate parasites and other bad actors that have systematically optimized large groups for malevolent purposes.
