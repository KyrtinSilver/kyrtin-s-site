551 - Institutionalized Gambling

June 8, 2026

The world is overflowing with forms of Gambling, although they often attempt to disassociate themselves from that term. This includes Stock Markets, other financial trading such as commodities, bonds, gold, crypto, etc. It also includes Insurance, Real Estate, and various overt forms of gambling like Casinos and Polymarket. Lastly, it includes virtually any system where a large sum of money is being invested in something whose future value remains dominantly uncertain, which includes most University degrees in countries like the US.

I’ll leave ranting about the absurdities of the broader economic structure to others, as it is truly and deeply absurd, requiring entire books to fully elaborate. Rather, lets focus on a different aspect of this.

The above examples of gambling can be divided into three primary categories:

  • Fancy-pants Gambling: Stock Markets, Real Estate, Commodities, Bonds, Cryptocurrency, Insurance, Debt-trading, etc.

  • Dice-Throwing-Chimpanzees: Casinos, Polymarket, and other overt forms of Gambling

  • Defunct and Decaying Institutions: Any sufficiently crippled institutional structure that no longer offers a dominant degree of certainty over whatever value it is intended to serve. This includes Universities in many countries, as well as their modern micro-derivatives, like “certifications”, as well as countless other institutions globally.

    *As institutional decay progresses and comorbidities accumulate, the number of such institutions grows.

To some limited degree, it isn’t possible to walk through life today without passing many bubbles of artificially inflated uncertainty. What you can be virtually certain of is that each such bubble is itself an ecological niche that has been carved out by parasites. While those parasites have a demonstrably advanced skill when it comes to camouflage, the structures that they build around them, to siphon off large amounts of resources for no productive purpose, place spotlights on them from this angle of perspective.

The “Fancy-pants Gambling” exerts by far the greatest influence on the world, while also clearly indicating that the lessons of the “Great Depression” in the US of the previous century were never truly learned. Even the much more recent “flash-crashes” were never explained, and yet the means that produced them weren’t set aside.

Those fancy forms of gambling are also EXTREMELY baked into the default requirements and options of ordinary life for most of the population in many countries. “Retirement Funds” are built on top of this Gambling, giving a huge portion of the population a critical point of failure, the potential for financial ruin, reliant on gambling.

Multiple (and increasing) varieties of “Insurance” are also often either legally required, and/or the basic services attached to them are rendered entirely unaffordable without it. While retirement funds are merely the default option that most people offered it may take, this goes a step further to legally mandate both the coercion of “protection money” (mafia style accounting), while also damaging whatever market it applies to, rendering that market damaged and unaffordable without it, like “Healthcare” in the US.

In contrast, Dice-Throwing-Chimpanzees are the pressure cooker where every available Dark Pattern may be applied to the lowest common denominators, while branding the activity as “entertainment”. This creates a space where bad actors may more freely and liberally experiment on the marginally-human, potentially coming up with new and more advanced Dark Patterns and forms of fraud to carve out new parasitic niches elsewhere in the world.

Defunct and Decaying Institutions offer a case study for the transition between some (at least partially) functional structure, to one where gambling and parasitic infection become the dominant forces. Universities are particularly well-studied for this, as researchers have closely observed and documented the rapid bloating of university “Administrators”, where the overwhelming majority of such roles to be added in the last 2 or 3 decades have been overtly parasitic. This is covered at some length in books like “The Coddling of the American Mind”, as well as the followup book “The Canceling of the American Mind”.

The concepts of the first category, Fancy-pants Gambling, are generally obscene perversions of some function that society is absolutely failing to deliver. Insurance is one of the more obvious of these, as some countries don’t operate on a basis of private insurance, lending direct (albeit very limited) contrast to existing examples. However, viable alternatives to all of those obscenities need to be architected, deployed, and their adoption “strongly encouraged”, even if that means a kick in the pants to those bloated pustules full of parasites.
